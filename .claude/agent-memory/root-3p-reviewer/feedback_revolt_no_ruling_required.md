---
name: Woodland Alliance revolt does not require ruling
description: Community sources sometimes imply revolts require ruling the clearing; the Law requires only a sympathetic clearing with no existing Alliance base.
type: feedback
---

Community sources (including spritesanddice--strategy-of-root.md, 2018) sometimes frame revolt as requiring the Alliance to "rule" or "control" a clearing, or advise opponents to "prevent revolts" by ensuring they rule clearings. The correct requirement (`faction:woodland$8.4.1.1`): the target must be a sympathetic clearing whose matching base type is still on the Alliance faction board. Ruling is not mentioned. The Alliance does not need to rule the clearing; the sympathy token itself plus two matching supporters is sufficient.

**Why:** The sympathy-first requirement makes revolts possible even in clearings densely held by opponents (making them particularly punishing). The Alliance's strength comes precisely from not needing to rule before revolting.

**How to apply:** When reviewing any Alliance-related source, flag any claim that revolts require the Alliance to rule or control the clearing. Also flag advice to Marquise/Eyrie players that holding rule of a clearing prevents revolt — it does not (though occupying it heavily raises the risk of Martial Law on sympathy placement, which does slow the path to revolt).

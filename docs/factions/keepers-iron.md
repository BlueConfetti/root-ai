# Keepers in Iron

## At a Glance

**Identity:** You score victory points by delving relics from forests, transporting
them to matching waystations, and recovering them — building a grow-as-you-act
Retinue engine that grants more actions the longer you survive without losing cards.

**Reach:** 3 (medium-low; requires time to build the engine, but 15 warriors spread
across the map create broad presence)

**Complexity:** High. Managing three interlocking decision trees — relic positioning,
Retinue card placement, and ruling requirements — at once demands forward planning
several turns ahead.

**Scoring tempo:** Slow-burn to burst. You accumulate little score in turns 1–3
while building your engine, then release a cascade of relic recoveries in mid-to-late
game.

**Setup constraints:** Choose a corner not occupied by another faction; prefer a
corner diagonally opposite an occupied one. See Section 3 below.

**Color:** Grey (hex `#8b8d90`)

**Faction icon:** `faction-keepers`

**No faction-level citation key.** Cite this faction via `rule:15.X.Y.Z`.

**Primary rule section:** `rule:15.`


## Win Condition

### Standard Route

Your primary scoring vehicle is relic recovery. Each of the twelve relic tokens has
a hidden value of 1, 2, or 3 — the distribution is one 1-value, one 2-value, and
two 3-values per relic type (`rule:15.2.1`), making the average relic worth
approximately 2.25 points. Recovering all twelve relics yields 27 raw points. Each time
you fill a column of four matching relics on your faction board — that is, you
complete a set of figures, tablets, or jewelry — you score an additional two points
(`rule:15.5.2.3.2`). Three completed columns add six more points, bringing the
theoretical maximum from relics alone to 33 — well past 30. In practice, opponents
will interfere with some relics or your ruling checks, so target recovering 8–10 relics
and supplementing with crafted items and piece removal to close out at 30.

### Dominance Path

You can activate a Dominance card during Daylight once you have 10 VP (`rule:3.3.1`).
Bird Dominance requires ruling two opposite corners; the suited Dominances require
ruling three clearings of one suit. Because your scoring engine is reliable and
accelerates over time, Dominance is rarely the right call — you are better positioned
to close out at 30 VP than to fight for positional lock. Reserve Dominance as a
desperation exit if the table is about to gang up and strip your relics before you
can recover them.

### Scoring Tempo

Turns 1–3 produce almost no VP. You are spending that time placing waystations, building
your Retinue, and positioning relics without triggering the discard checks that would
cost you cards. The payoff arrives in turns 4–8, when a fully built Retinue can produce
6–10 VP in a single round through chained recoveries. Opponents who do not act to disrupt
you before that window opens are likely to lose.


## Setup

### Step-by-Step

1. **Place starting relics.** Collect all twelve relic tokens, shuffle them face down,
   and place one randomly in each forest (`rule:15.3.1`).

2. **Gather warriors.** Form a supply of 15 warriors (`rule:15.3.`).

3. **Place warriors.** Choose a corner clearing not occupied by another faction,
   preferring a position diagonally opposite a taken corner. Place four warriors in
   that corner. Then place four more warriors in the map-edge clearing adjacent to
   it (`rule:15.3.`). You start with eight warriors on the board and seven in reserve.

4. **Place remaining relics.** Distribute the remaining relics randomly among forests
   not adjacent to clearings where you placed warriors (`rule:15.3.3`). These relics
   stay face down until delved.

5. **Tuck Faithful Retainers.** Tuck one Faithful Retainer card (`card:ROOT-13
   (Marauders)`) into each Retinue slot — one in each of the three columns (`rule:15.3.`).

6. **Collect waystations.** Place your three waystation buildings on the Waystations
   spaces of your faction board (`rule:15.3.`). They start off the map.

### Setup Theory

**Corner choice.** Prefer the corner most isolated from aggressive factions. Your
first few turns require you to expand toward relics without being immediately contested.
A corner diagonal to the Marquise or Eyrie is workable if you can claim the adjacent
map-edge clearing; avoid corners boxed in between two early-game builders who will
contest every clearing before you can encamp.

**Relic proximity matters.** Your starting warriors sit in two adjacent clearings.
Forests adjacent to those clearings are off-limits for the second relic placement step
(`rule:15.3.3`). More distance between your warriors and the bulk of the relics is a
mild disadvantage — you will need more movement actions to reach them. On maps with
many forests (the Autumn map, the Mountain map), relics distribute widely and you may
need to plan multiple delve corridors. On tighter maps, a single well-chosen waystation
corridor can cover most of the relic field.

**Neighbor considerations.** Woodland Alliance is the most dangerous neighbor at setup
time: Sympathy tokens in your clearings feed their Supporters and restrict your movement
options (`rule:8.2.5`, `rule:8.2.6`). Place far from the Alliance's starting position if
the choice is available. The Marquise de Cat's broad early expansion can crowd out the
clearings you need to rule for delving and recovery; distance helps here too.

**Opening hand.** Your hand serves three functions on turn 1: recruiting warriors to
waystations (`rule:15.4.3`), topping up your Retinue (`rule:15.6.2`), and eventual
crafting. Bird cards are the most flexible both for recruiting (they match any suited
waystation) and for Retinue placement. Keep birds and one suited card if possible.
Discard redundant cards of a suit you do not yet have a waystation for.


## Faction Rules and Abilities

### Relics

You have twelve relic tokens split across three types: figures, tablets, and jewelry
(`rule:15.2.1`). Each type has four tokens. The face-down side shows only the type;
the face-up side (revealed when you delve) shows a value of 1, 2, 3, or 3. Two of the
four relics per type are worth 3 points. Because values are hidden until delved, you
cannot know which relics are worth more until you flip them in a forest adjacent to your
delving clearing.

Relics begin in forests — spaces between clearings that hold no buildings or tokens and
are not directly entered by warriors. A relic in a forest is not subject to battle. Only
the Keepers' delve action can move a relic out of a forest. Once in a clearing, relics
can be moved by Keeper warriors using the Devout Knights ability, and can be removed by
enemies triggering Prized Trophies.

### Waystations

You have three waystations — double-sided buildings (`rule:15.2.2`). Each side shows one
relic type. The front and back of a single waystation show two different types, giving
you flexibility to choose which type to present when you encamp. A clearing with a
waystation of a given type is the destination for relics of that type during recovery.
You need a matching waystation present in a clearing to recover a relic there
(`rule:15.5.2.3.1`).

Waystations also function as your crafting pieces: you activate any waystation on the
map to satisfy crafting requirements, regardless of the clearing's suit (`rule:15.2.3`).
This unusual crafting rule means three waystations on the board equals three crafting
activations per Daylight — a significant crafting ceiling compared to most factions.

Waystations are buildings, so they occupy building slots (`rule:2.2.3`). When encamping,
you replace a warrior with a waystation in that clearing (`rule:15.4.1`). Because
waystations take slot space, dense clearings on some maps may limit your options.

### Devout Knights

Whenever you enter battle in a clearing that contains at least one relic and at least one
Keeper warrior, you ignore the first hit you take in that battle (`rule:15.2.4`). This is
a defensive bonus, not an attacking one — it applies when you are attacked and when you
initiate battle. The hit-ignore does not stack: if multiple relics are present, you still
ignore only one hit.

If you are ambushed, you ignore one hit from the ambush itself, but subsequent hits in
the same battle are not ignored (`rule:15.2.4`). Keep a relic in a contested clearing
to make your warriors meaningfully harder to dislodge without spending extra cards.

The second half of Devout Knights governs relic movement: when you move warriors between
clearings, you may move one relic alongside each warrior that moves (`rule:15.2.4`). A
group move of four warriors from clearing A to clearing B can bring up to four relics
with it. This is how you transport delved relics to waystation clearings without spending
Retinue actions.

### Prized Trophies

Whenever an enemy removes a relic, they place it in any forest face up and score an
extra victory point — for a total of two VP from the removal (`rule:15.2.5`). The first
VP comes from the standard rule for removing enemy tokens (`rule:3.2.1`); the second VP
is the Prized Trophies bonus. This mechanic punishes opponents for destroying your relics
carelessly: they gain points, yes, but the relic re-enters the map face up, which means
you can see its value and potentially reclaim it.

Hirelings that remove a relic do not trigger VP awards in either direction — no VP is
scored, and placement of the relic falls to the controlling player (or the active player
if uncontrolled) per the FAQ (`faq:hirelings-relic-removal`). Vagabond pieces in
coalition with the Keepers do not trigger Prized Trophies since they are not enemies in
that case (`faq:vagrant-coalition-relic`).

### Faithful Retainer

The Faithful Retainer (`card:ROOT-13 (Marauders)`) is a special card that starts in each
of your three Retinue columns. Its text reads: "When you discard this card, remove it
permanently." If a Faithful Retainer is discarded — whether by a failed discard check
during delve or recover — it leaves the game. You cannot replace it with another
Faithful Retainer. This means your starting action-count baseline is fragile: lose a
Faithful Retainer early and you permanently lose one action per round in that column
until you fill the slot with a drawn card.

Protect your Faithful Retainers by never triggering the discard check unless you are
confident you will pass it. The check for delve: you must rule at least as many adjacent
clearings as the relic's value (`rule:15.5.2.2.2`). The check for recover: you must rule
at least as many same-suit clearings as the relic's value (`rule:15.5.2.3.3`).


## Turn Structure

### Birdsong

Birdsong runs three steps in order: Encamp, Decamp, then Recruit (`rule:15.4.`).

**Encamp** (`rule:15.4.1`): Once per clearing, replace a Keeper warrior with a waystation.
You choose which face to present (i.e., which relic type the waystation shows). You can
encamp in multiple clearings in the same Birdsong, once each. If you have no warriors
or waystations anywhere on the map, you may instead place one waystation and one warrior
in any map-edge clearing — a recovery clause for catastrophic losses. Encamping converts
board presence (a warrior) into infrastructure (a waystation) and costs you a warrior slot.

**Decamp** (`rule:15.4.2`): Once per clearing, replace a waystation with a Keeper warrior.
Decamping retrieves a waystation from the map back to your board, replacing it with a
warrior. You might decamp to relocate a waystation to a better position next Birdsong,
or to pack up a waystation whose relic type has already been fully recovered.

**Recruit** (`rule:15.4.3`): Spend any number of cards to place warriors. Each card
spent places two warriors at a waystation whose clearing suit matches that card's suit.
Bird cards (`rule:15.4.3`) match any suit. Recruiting is your primary way to replenish
warriors lost to Live Off the Land (`rule:15.6.1`) and battle. You need warriors on the
board to rule clearings — and you need to rule clearings to pass delve and recover checks.

Note that Recruit requires a matching waystation on the map. If all waystations are on
your faction board (not yet placed), you cannot recruit. Get at least one waystation
onto the map before worrying about Retinue construction.

### Daylight

Daylight has two steps in order: Craft, then Act with Retinue (`rule:15.5.`).

**Craft** (`rule:15.5.1`): Activate any waystations on the map, regardless of their suit,
to pay crafting costs. Each waystation can be activated once per turn. With three
waystations on the map, you can satisfy up to three single-suit crafting requirements or
one three-suit requirement. This is a strong crafting position — more flexible than the
Marquise (which requires specific buildings by suit) and more consistent than the Eyrie.

**Act with Retinue** (`rule:15.5.2`): Starting from the leftmost column and moving right,
you may take the column's action once for each card in that column, in any order within
the column. The three columns are:

- **Move (leftmost)** (`rule:15.5.2.1`): Each card in this column lets you take one move
  from a clearing whose suit matches the card's suit. Bird cards here match any suit.
  You never lose cards from the Move column; there is no discard check.

- **Battle then Delve (middle)** (`rule:15.5.2.2`): Choose a clearing matching the card's
  suit. If enemy pieces that you can battle are present, you must battle them first
  (`rule:15.5.2.2` — "must"). Then, if you rule the clearing and it has at least one
  Keeper warrior, you may delve: flip a relic in an adjacent forest to reveal its value
  and move it into the clearing. After delving, check: if you rule fewer adjacent
  clearings than the relic's value, discard the Retinue card (`rule:15.5.2.2.2`).

- **Move or Recover (rightmost)** (`rule:15.5.2.3`): Choose a matching clearing. Take
  a move from it, or recover relics. To recover: remove a relic matching a waystation
  type present in that clearing, score VP equal to the relic's value, score +2 VP if
  you filled a column, then check: if you rule fewer same-suit clearings than the
  relic's value, discard the Retinue card (`rule:15.5.2.3.3`). If you pass the check,
  you may take another relic from the same clearing and repeat.

**Column order is mandatory** (`rule:15.5.2`). You resolve all Move column cards before
any Battle-then-Delve cards, and all Battle-then-Delve cards before any Move-or-Recover
cards. Within a column you choose the order freely.

### Evening

Evening runs three steps in order: Live Off the Land, Gather Retinue, then Draw and
Discard (`rule:15.6.`).

**Live Off the Land** (`rule:15.6.1`): You must remove one of your own warriors from
each clearing where you have four or more Keeper warriors. This is not optional. It
prevents you from stacking large forces in single clearings and incentivizes spreading
warriors broadly. Use it to your advantage: leave exactly three warriors in any clearing
you want to hold firmly, and let the trimmed warrior go somewhere useful next turn.

**Gather Retinue** (`rule:15.6.2`): Add any number of cards from your hand to any
Retinue slots, or shift one card already in the Retinue to a different slot (one shift
per Evening). Your Retinue cannot exceed ten cards total. This is your primary engine-
building step. Earlier placement means earlier use — a card in your Retinue on turn 2
has already acted on turn 3, while a card placed on turn 5 first acts on turn 6. Loading
your Retinue early is almost always correct, subject to the discard-check risk discussed
in Section 7.

**Draw and Discard** (`rule:15.6.3`): Draw one card plus one card per waystation
currently on the map. With three waystations on the board, you draw four cards per
Evening — a generous hand replenishment rate that fuels both Recruiting and continued
Retinue growth. Then discard down to five cards if needed.


## Scoring Engine

| VP Source | Amount | Trigger | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Relic recovery | 1–3 per relic | Move or Recover action | Daylight (Retinue) |
| Column completion bonus | 2 per set | Fourth relic of a type recovered | Daylight (Retinue) |
| Crafted items | Varies by card | Craft step | Daylight (Craft) |
| Enemy building/token removal | 1 per piece | Battle or card effect | Daylight (Retinue or card) |
| Prized Trophies (enemy triggers) | 2 total to enemy | Enemy removes a relic | Not your turn — this scores for your opponent |

**Primary engine:** Relic recovery. Recovering all 12 relics at average value ~2.25 yields
27 VP. Three completed columns add 6 VP for a potential 33 from relics alone — more than
enough to win. In competitive play, expect to recover 8–10 relics while supplementing with
crafted items and piece removal, reaching 30 VP around turns 7–10.

**Card draw as multiplier:** Every waystation on the map adds one card per draw step
(`rule:15.6.3`). More cards means faster Retinue growth means more Daylight actions.
Getting all three waystations onto the map quickly is the single biggest accelerator of
your scoring engine.

**Secondary engine:** Crafting. With up to three waystations as crafting pieces, you can
craft higher-cost cards that most factions struggle to reach. Items worth 3 VP
(`item:sword`, `item:crossbow`) are plausible targets once your waystation infrastructure
is in place. Persistent-effect cards that generate ongoing bonuses (Cobbler for extra
moves, Tax Collector for extra card draw) extend your reach further.

**Opponent scoring for you:** Prized Trophies (`rule:15.2.5`) means enemies who casually
destroy your relics give themselves VP, not you. The mechanic creates a deterrent but
does not net you any VP — do not factor it into your own score projections.


## Playbook

### Opening (Turns 1–3)

Your first priority is infrastructure: get two or three waystations onto the map. Every
waystation on the map accelerates your card draw, and card draw fuels everything else. In
turn 1, Encamp at least one waystation in a clearing you already rule, then spend cards
in Recruit to put warriors at that waystation. Do not try to delve yet unless you are
certain the relic in the adjacent forest is worth 1 VP and you rule enough adjacent
clearings to pass the check — losing a Faithful Retainer in turn 1 is a game-altering
setback.

A reasonable turn-1 sequence: Encamp at your starting corner (converting one of your
four warriors to a waystation), Recruit two warriors back at that waystation using a
matching card, add two cards to your Retinue in Evening, then draw two cards (one base
plus one for the waystation).

Turn 2, Encamp a second waystation in the adjacent map-edge clearing. You now draw three
cards per Evening. Use Daylight to move toward forests that hold relics you want, taking
Retinue Move actions.

Turn 3, look to get your third waystation down. At this point your Evening draw is four
cards. Add aggressively to your Retinue — aim for 7–8 cards by the end of turn 3. On
maps where your starting position sits between multiple forests, you may be able to delve
a 1-value relic safely this turn if your ruling position covers all forests adjacent to the
chosen clearing.

**Opening hand priorities:** Keep bird cards for Recruit flexibility and Retinue
placement in the Move or Move-or-Recover columns. Keep suited cards matching your starting
clearings for Retinue placement in the Battle-then-Delve column. Discard excess cards of
suits far from your starting position.

### Mid-Game Pivots

By turns 4–5, your Retinue should have 7–10 cards and your three waystations should be
positioned to cover at least two relic types. Now is when you begin delving and recovering
in earnest.

**Read the board before each delve.** Before using a Battle-then-Delve card, count the
clearings you rule adjacent to each candidate forest. A 3-value relic requires you to
rule at least three of those clearings. If you cannot, either pass on that forest (move
the action to a different clearing) or flip the relic first to confirm it is a low-value
one before committing.

**If the table is threatening your ruling positions:** Prioritize recovering lower-value
relics quickly rather than maximizing per-recovery VP. A 1-VP recovery you can chain
repeatedly without discarding cards beats a 3-VP recovery that costs you a Retinue card.

**If an opponent is building a dominant board position** (Marquise approaching full
building coverage, Eyrie with a full Decree and unchallenged roosts): pivot to remove
their buildings and tokens during your Battle-then-Delve actions to slow their scoring.
You earn 1 VP per piece removed (`rule:3.2.1`) and maintain board relevance without
abandoning relic work.

**Waystation repositioning.** If you have already recovered all relics of one type, the
waystation showing that type is no longer useful on its primary side. Flip it to show the
other type via Decamp and re-Encamp, or decamp it to increase your hand size next Evening.
Flexible waystation management distinguishes experienced Keepers players from new ones.

### Endgame Routes

When you have 20+ VP and 2–3 columns complete, you are in closing range. The two main
routes:

**Column completion burst.** If you have one column close to complete (three relics
recovered), position a Move-or-Recover card for the matching suit and stack a clearing
near the final relic. Recovering the last relic earns its base value plus the two-point
column bonus in a single action.

**Chained recovery turn.** A fully built Retinue with multiple Move-or-Recover cards can
recover several relics in a single Daylight if you rule enough same-suit clearings. Position
two or more relics of the same type in a clearing with a matching waystation. Each recovery
that passes the ruling check lets you return to the "Take Relic" step (`rule:15.5.2.3.3`).
Three relics of one type recovered in one action sequence yields 7–9 VP plus potentially
the column bonus.

**Board control finish.** If relics are scarce (many have been removed by enemies or
there are fewer remaining than you need), lean on crafted items and piece removal to close
the remaining gap. Royal Claim (`card:ROOT-92 (Base Game)`) discarded in Birdsong scores
1 VP per ruling — if you rule 5–6 clearings, it can cover 5–6 VP in one card spend. Tax
Collector (`card:ROOT-105 (Base Game)`) provides ongoing draw at the cost of a warrior,
which can accelerate Retinue loading in a pinch.


## Threat Factions

### Woodland Alliance

The Alliance's Outrage (`rule:8.2.5`) triggers every time you move warriors into a
sympathetic clearing. Because your strategy requires you to move warriors widely across
the map to rule clearings for delve and recover checks, you will constantly be at risk
of feeding the Alliance's Supporters stack. Each Outrage gives them a card and accelerates
their Revolt engine.

More pressingly, the Alliance's Revolt (`rule:8.4.1`) places Bases and fills clearings
with Allied warriors. A Base in a clearing you need to rule for recovery disrupts your
ruling check and forces a battle. Battling the Alliance is expensive: their Guerrilla
Warfare (`rule:8.2.2`) swaps attack and defense rolls, meaning they deal more hits than
normal when defending.

Counter-play: Before the Alliance builds momentum, station three or more warriors in
clearings you cannot afford to lose. The Martial Law rule (`rule:8.4.2.2.1`) requires the
Alliance to spend an extra supporter to place Sympathy in a clearing with three or more
enemy warriors — a meaningful additional cost that slows their spread. If a Base appears,
remove it immediately — the Alliance's VP engine stalls without Bases and removing a base
strips their officers and Supporters. Your Devout Knights bonus (`rule:15.2.4`) is
irrelevant against the Alliance since Sympathy tokens cannot trigger it (no relic is
typically present in those clearings); rely on superior warrior counts.

### Corvid Conspiracy

The Corvids' Plot tokens (`rule:13.2.2`) can appear anywhere. Their Bomb plot
(`rule:13.7.1`) removes all enemy pieces in the clearing when flipped — losing warriors
in key clearings directly fails your delve and recover ruling checks. Their Extortion
plot (`rule:13.7.3`) takes random cards from enemies in the clearing when flipped,
draining your hand and slowing Retinue growth. Raid plots (`rule:13.7.4`) place Corvid
warriors in adjacent clearings when removed, potentially flooding clearings you need
to rule.

Their Exposure (`rule:13.2.4`) mechanic lets opponents (including you) show the Corvids
a matching card to guess a face-down plot's type. If correct, you remove the plot and
score 1 VP. Use Exposure offensively when you see a plot in a clearing adjacent to your
delve forest — if it is an Extortion token, removing it costs the Corvids card draw and
removes the threat before it fires on their turn.

Counter-play: Keep warriors in small, spread groups rather than concentrating forces. A
single warrior in a cleared clearing does not give the Corvids a juicy target, and
spreading across the map means no single Raid devastates your ruling structure. Attack
Corvid clearings where their warrior count is low; the Corvids typically run thin.

### Lord of the Hundreds

The Hundreds' Oppress mechanic (`rule:14.6.2`) scores them VP per turn for clearings
they rule without enemy pieces. Because you move warriors broadly and your Live Off the
Land mechanic trims stacks (`rule:15.6.1`), you may leave clearings temporarily empty —
exactly the conditions the Hundreds exploit. Their Warlord's presence (`rule:14.`) also
makes contested clearings costly for you: the Warlord's battle advantages can disrupt
your Battle-then-Delve actions.

Counter-play: Accept that you will contest fewer clearings than usual against the Hundreds.
Focus on maintaining ruling presence in the specific clearings needed for your delve and
recover checks rather than trying to hold the whole board. The Hundreds score passively;
reduce their score rate by keeping at least one warrior in clearings they would otherwise
oppress. Their Mob tokens (`rule:14.`) occupy building slots, which can work in your favor
by limiting where they can entrench — your waystations are not in those positions.

### Eyrie Dynasties

The Eyrie's Lords of the Forest (`rule:7.2.2`) gives them rule of every tied clearing,
which directly contests your ruling requirements for delve and recover checks. A single
Eyrie roost can match your warrior count and strip rule from you without a battle, costing
you the actions you planned for that clearing.

The Eyrie also scores fast (`rule:7.`) — consistently 3–6 VP per turn from roost scoring
— meaning they may reach 30 VP before you complete your relic engine if left unchecked.

Counter-play: Attack Eyrie roosts in your key clearings during Battle-then-Delve actions.
Each roost destroyed reduces the number of roosts on the map, which lowers the VP the
Eyrie scores in their next Evening (`rule:7.6.1`). Removing roosts from clearings you
need for recovery solves both problems at once — you clear the ruling contest and slow
their scoring. The Eyrie dislikes Turmoil and will often avoid battle when their Decree
is close to the edge; use that caution against them.


## Card Priorities

### Top Priorities (Craft or Keep)

**Cobbler** (`card:ROOT-60 (Base Game)`, Bunny, persistent): Gives you a free move at
the start of Evening, before your Evening steps begin. This extends your reach
significantly — you can reposition for the following turn's ruling checks without
spending a Move column card. Worth crafting whenever you have a Bunny-matched waystation
with a slot free.

**Tax Collector** (`card:ROOT-105 (Base Game)`, Fox, persistent): Remove one of your
warriors to draw a card, once per Daylight. Given Live Off the Land trims your warriors
anyway, converting a doomed warrior into a card is efficient. Feeds Retinue loading.

**Brutal Tactics** (`card:ROOT-58 (Base Game)`, Bird, persistent): Lets you deal an
extra hit as attacker but gives the defender 1 VP. Your Battle-then-Delve column must
battle if enemies are present (`rule:15.5.2.2`). When forced into battle, having an
extra hit prevents you from being stuck against large defensive stacks.

**Royal Claim** (`card:ROOT-92 (Base Game)`, Bird, immediate): Score 1 VP per clearing
you rule. In mid-game when you rule 5–7 clearings for your delve/recovery infrastructure,
this card is a 5–7 VP spike at the cost of one card. Save for the turn when you expect
the most board presence, ideally during a closing push.

**Armorers** (`card:ROOT-51 (Base Game)`, Bird, persistent): In battle, discard to ignore
all rolled hits. Strong insurance against being wiped out of a relic-bearing clearing.
The discard cost means you plan for one use; place it in your hand until you need it.

**Better Burrow Bank** (`card:ROOT-55 (Base Game)`, Bunny, persistent): At Birdsong,
optionally draw one card and give an enemy one card. The conditional draw is useful for
keeping your hand full and Retinue fed even when relics are scarce.

### Situational

**Scouting Party** (`card:ROOT-95 (Base Game)`, Mouse, persistent): Ignore ambush cards
as attacker. Useful if you frequently initiate battles in your Battle-then-Delve column
against factions that hold ambush cards. Less valuable if the table is ambush-light.

**Sappers** (`card:ROOT-94 (Base Game)`, Bird, persistent): Deal an extra hit as defender.
Your Devout Knights ability (`rule:15.2.4`) already ignores the first hit when a relic is
present. Sappers stacks on top of that in a relic-bearing clearing, making you extremely
resilient on defense. Situationally powerful if enemies are regularly attacking you.

**Command Warren** (`card:ROOT-63 (Base Game)`, Bunny, persistent): Initiate a battle at
the start of Daylight, before your Retinue actions. Useful if you need to clear a clearing
for a subsequent delve but the Battle-then-Delve column does not match the clearing's suit.

**Favor cards** (`card:ROOT-73 (Base Game)`, `card:ROOT-74 (Base Game)`,
`card:ROOT-75 (Base Game)`, immediate): Remove all enemy pieces in all clearings of one
suit. Devastating against factions clustered in one suit, and can clear a path for your
recovery actions in a single card spend. High cost relative to typical use; most valuable
in one-turn closing plays.

### Ambush Guidance

Hold at least one Ambush card (`card:ROOT-46 (Base Game)` or matching suit) through the
mid-game. Your Devout Knights ability ignores the first hit you take from an ambush
(`rule:15.2.4`), which means an opponent who ambushes you in a relic-bearing clearing
only deals one hit net — but you want to hold your own ambush as a deterrent or to cancel
theirs when needed.

### Dominance Cards

Activating Dominance (`rule:3.3.1`) removes your VP marker and forces a positional win
condition. Given your scoring engine's natural trajectory toward 30 VP, Dominance is
rarely correct. Consider it only if your relic supply is essentially depleted (many relics
removed and scattered face up in hard-to-reach forests) and you control enough clearings
to fulfill the condition reliably. Bird Dominance (ruling two opposite corners) is the
most achievable, as your setup corner plus one expansion toward an opposite corner is a
natural movement path.


## Common Pitfalls

- **Delving before ruling coverage is secure.** The discard check (`rule:15.5.2.2.2`)
  destroys Faithful Retainers permanently. If you cannot rule the required number of
  adjacent clearings, do not delve. Every premature delve that costs a Retinue card
  permanently reduces your action count.

- **Neglecting waystation placement in turns 1–2.** Each waystation on the map adds one
  card to your Evening draw (`rule:15.6.3`). Delaying waystation placement by even one
  turn compounds into fewer cards in hand, slower Retinue growth, and fewer recruits.
  Getting all three waystations onto the map is the most time-sensitive early-game task.

- **Clustering warriors.** Live Off the Land (`rule:15.6.1`) removes one warrior from
  every clearing with four or more Keepers. Stacking five warriors in one clearing and
  then fighting costs you warriors to the rule before battle even happens. Keep stacks at
  three or fewer unless you are deliberately bulking up for a clearing contest.

- **Misreading the recover check.** The recover check counts clearings you rule whose
  suit matches the clearing you are recovering from (`rule:15.5.2.3.3`) — not adjacent
  clearings. This is different from the delve check (`rule:15.5.2.2.2`), which counts
  adjacent clearings. Mixing up these two different ruling requirements is one of the
  most common rules errors and leads to losing Retinue cards unnecessarily.

- **Leaving relics in clearings you cannot defend.** A relic in an uncontested clearing
  you do not rule gives enemies a free 2-VP pop by removing it (`rule:15.2.5`). Do not
  delve a relic into a clearing unless you can maintain enough warriors there to make
  removal costly.

- **Overfilling the Retinue before ruling infrastructure exists.** Adding ten cards to
  your Retinue in turns 1–2 feels powerful, but if you don't rule enough clearings to
  pass the discard checks, those cards will burn off during forced delves. Build your
  ruling network — warrior positions, waystations placed, clearings controlled — before
  aggressively loading actions you cannot safely take.

- **Ignoring the mandatory battle in Battle-then-Delve.** The text reads "You must
  initiate a battle there if any enemy pieces that you can battle are there"
  (`rule:15.5.2.2`). You cannot skip the battle step to proceed directly to delve. Plan
  around this: if a clearing you want to delve from is contested, you will fight before
  delving. Have warriors in reserve or use a Move action first to clear the way.

- **Forgetting that column order is mandatory.** You must complete all Move column actions
  before any Battle-then-Delve actions, and all Battle-then-Delve actions before any
  Move-or-Recover actions (`rule:15.5.2`). Planning a recovery before having moved
  warriors into position means the warriors arrive during Move column, then you can
  recover during the Move-or-Recover column in the same turn. Do not plan a turn that
  requires out-of-order column execution.


## Mechanic Clarifications

### Does the Battle-then-Delve action require you to rule before battling?

No. You must rule the clearing to delve, but you do not need to rule in order to initiate
the battle itself. The battle happens first; if the battle results in you ruling the clearing,
you may then delve. This distinction is specifically confirmed by the FAQ
(`faq:rule-to-delve-not-battle`).

**Citation:** `rule:15.5.2.2`; `faq:rule-to-delve-not-battle`

### Are relic placement steps randomized, or can the Keepers choose positions?

Both relic placement steps are randomized. The Keepers cannot choose which relics go in
which forests during either setup step (`faq:relic-placement-random`). The only input is
that the second batch avoids forests adjacent to starting warrior clearings (`rule:15.3.3`).

**Citation:** `rule:15.3.1`; `rule:15.3.3`; `faq:relic-placement-random`

### Can you flip a relic's value face up in the forest without delving it?

No. The only mechanism for revealing a relic's value is the delve action's first step:
"Flip a relic in an adjacent forest so its value is showing if it is not, then move it
into the chosen clearing" (`rule:15.5.2.2.1`). Flipping and moving are inseparable — you
cannot flip a relic to scout its value and then decline to move it.

**Citation:** `rule:15.5.2.2.1`

### If a hireling removes a relic, does anyone score Prized Trophies VP?

No VP is awarded to anyone. If the hireling is controlled, the controlling player decides
where to place the relic. If uncontrolled, the active player decides. The Prized Trophies
text says "Whenever an enemy removes a relic" — hirelings are not faction pieces of the
controlling player (`faq:hirelings-relic-removal`).

**Citation:** `rule:15.2.5`; `faq:hirelings-relic-removal`

### Can you recover multiple relics of the same type in one Move-or-Recover action?

Yes, if you pass each discard check. After recovering one relic, if you passed the check
(ruling enough same-suit clearings), you may return to the Take Relic step and recover
another relic from the same clearing, provided there is still a relic there matching the
waystation type (`rule:15.5.2.3.3`). You continue until you fail a check or choose to
end the action.

**Citation:** `rule:15.5.2.3.3`

### Do you move relics with every warrior that moves, or just some of them?

You may move one relic per warrior that moves, but it is optional per warrior. You could
choose to have three of four moving warriors carry a relic and the fourth move without one.
The rule says "may move one relic with each Keeper warrior that they move" (`rule:15.2.4`).

**Citation:** `rule:15.2.4`

### Can you encamp or decamp more than once per clearing per Birdsong?

No. Encamp is "once per clearing" and Decamp is "once per clearing" per Birdsong
(`rule:15.4.1`, `rule:15.4.2`). You can encamp in different clearings in the same
Birdsong, but not the same clearing twice.

**Citation:** `rule:15.4.1`; `rule:15.4.2`

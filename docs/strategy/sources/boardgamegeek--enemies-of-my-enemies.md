# Distilled

## General

### Scoring Curve Types
Four distinct scoring patterns define faction tempo:

- **Steady**: Marquise — approximately 3 VP/turn at a constant rate. Gets outpaced by everyone else.
- **Linear-Steady**: Eyrie — approximately 3.5 VP/turn after a short buildup. Ahead of Marquise; behind exponential factions.
- **Exponential**: Woodland Alliance, Vagabond, Lizard Cult — low early points, massive late acceleration. These factions are the game's natural winners if unchecked.
- **Burst**: Riverfolk — limited by items available to craft, trade posts, and Favor card timing. Must generate a big point burst in final 2 rounds.

### Faction Archetypes
- **Armies (Counterinsurgents)**: Marquise, Eyrie, Lizard Cult — large warrior counts, rule large board areas. Their strategic role is policing exponential factions.
- **Insurgents**: Woodland Alliance, Vagabond — minimal offensive power; score through presence and positioning.
- **Hybrids**: Riverfolk — can fill either role depending on game composition.

### Macro Game Logic
The game pace is set by the Marquise and Eyrie. It is in their interest to fight exponential factions (Vagabond, Woodland Alliance), not each other. In recommended setups (2+ Armies), Armies form a de facto coalition to contain Insurgents. If one Army is absent, whoever takes its policing role changes dramatically.

### Crafting Policy
- Crafting in Mice: immunity to Ambushes.
- Crafting in Rabbits: extra actions.
- Crafting in Foxes: army efficiency.
- Avoid crafting Hammers and Swords that benefit the Vagabond. Do not give the Vagabond their first Tea (refreshes 5 items) or second Boots (moves through hostile territory).

### Dominance Victories
Difficult and requires the stars to align. Requires: Marquise short on warriors, Eyrie turmoiled or low on warriors, Woodland Alliance unable to revolt against you, Vagabond forced to repair, Lizard Cult unable to threaten Conspiracies, Riverfolk unable to generate army. Despite the challenge, Marquise and Eyrie often rely on Dominance because their point scoring lags insurgent factions.

## Marquise de Cat

### Win Condition
Scoring is possible but requires sustained Workshop + crafting investment. Dominance is the more viable path — requires partnering with the opposite-corner player to suppress exponential factions.

### Playbook
Craft in Rabbits for action economy. Hoard Ambush cards. Build Sawmills and Recruiters primarily; Workshops for points. Craft items only after turn 5–6 when Vagabond is contained.

### Counterplay
Other factions mostly outrace Marquise on points. The table strategy is: make friends with Marquise, let them police your enemies, then outscore them late. A Favor card dropped on Marquise can cripple their entire economy.

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

### Win Condition
Four leader paths: Charismatic (early warriors), Despot + Commander (military), Builder (late points). Dominance is risky but plausible given ruling-tie advantage. Color cards in Recruit/Move, Bird cards for Battle/Build.

### Playbook
Set up early game carefully. Plan to turmoil at most once, intentionally, when you can maximize benefit from the reset. Turmoiling twice is near-fatal. The Commander is good for attacking Alliance bases with 1–2 hit swings.

### Counterplay
Force turmoil by denying colored Recruit clearings or moving out of Battle areas. Most damaging windows: early turmoil before Eyrie gets max benefit, or forcing turmoil when Despot/Commander could have otherwise scored big.

---

## Woodland Alliance

### Win Condition
Exponential scorer — spread Sympathy early, establish base turn 2, then party. Keep supporter pile free of base-suit cards to protect against invasion.

### Playbook
Get spread of Sympathy on turn 1 away from enemies. Base on turn 2. Aim for 4–7 officers. 7-warrior-in-box play is extreme all-or-nothing; 4–5 officers is standard. If a base is destroyed, build another elsewhere.

### Counterplay
Restrict Sympathy to one corner, then apply Rule over bases to prevent moving and Organizing. Stack 3+ warriors for Martial Law. Smash Sympathy that threatens valuable clearings; let harmless Sympathy sit. Running Rule on the base forces extra Recruit actions from the Alliance, slowing their tempo.

---

## Vagabond

### Win Condition
Multiple paths: Aid for points, Quests, hostile battle (warriors + buildings), Coalition. Exponential scorer — slow early, then near-unstoppable by turn 5–6 once items are established.

### Playbook
Sword and Hammer are mandatory early. If Hammer doesn't appear by turn 4, pivot: two Swords can generate 4+ points/turn on hostilities; Alliance Coalition gives huge point gain; Quests stack up. Even without Hammer, a Torch can clear ruins and maintain function.

### Counterplay
Attack Vagabond within the first four turns — making them retreat twice is enough to set their points back to pace with everyone else. The earlier they become Hostile, the costlier their movement. Never craft Hammers, Swords, Teas (refreshes 5 items), second Boots, or Bags for them.

---

## Lizard Cult

### Win Condition
Exponential scorer. Two gardens per suit, then score every turn. Conspiracies provide both offense (convert/crusade) and defense (protect gardens). Dominance is viable — opponents must literally kill all warriors to contest your clearings.

### Playbook
Build gardens in two of each suit. Use Conspiracies offensively to convert Riverfolk/Alliance warriors to free your Army partner. Pay attention to Ambush and Favor draws for timing attacks on poorly defended gardens.

### Counterplay
Wipe gardens when poorly defended. Every warrior you kill generates a Conspiracy action — commit only when you can remove an entire garden (or two), otherwise you're just feeding their action economy.

---

## Riverfolk Company

### Win Condition
Burst scorer. Price competitively — never rely on Dividends alone. Eyrie and Marquise are best customers for permanent upgrades and Bird cards. Stay relevant to multiple factions.

### Playbook
Price Hand Cards low enough to generate purchases; price high for key Ambushes/Favors. Keep Trade Posts for crafting. If someone isn't buying, stay under the leader in VPs and time a burst in the final rounds.

### Counterplay
Always buy at least 1–2 funds of services before their turn so opponents receive some benefit rather than letting Riverfolk monopolize the value. Do not destroy Trade Posts (costs 1 VP to you, free replacement for them). Kill warriors instead. On turn 5 they should have ~12 funds; turn 10 ~25 funds — breaking those thresholds is critical. Think of Riverfolk as a slightly slower, more stable Vagabond.

# Key Takeaways

- **Exponential factions (Woodland Alliance, Vagabond, Lizard Cult) win by default if unchecked** — the armies must police them.
- **Marquise and Eyrie should not fight each other** — neither scores fast enough to compensate; both need each other to contain Insurgents.
- **Vagabond must be hit by turn 4** — after that, items make them increasingly unstoppable.
- **Never craft Hammers or Swords** — both benefit Vagabond disproportionately and are almost never worth it for crafting faction.
- **Eyrie should turmoil at most once, intentionally** — turmoiling twice is near-fatal in terms of point recovery.
- **Woodland Alliance scoring is deceptive** — 2 warriors and 4 sympathy can threaten multiple revolts; evaluate mechanics not piece count.
- **Riverfolk are beat by killing warriors, not Trade Posts** — destroying Trade Posts is a free replacement; warriors require funds to replace.
- **Game era structure**: early (turns 1–4), mid (3–7), end (8–10). Set up so nobody holds a significant advantage entering mid-game.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 6/10
**Verdict:** Conceptual framework (scoring curves, faction archetypes, macro logic) is durable and largely sound, but one material Law contradiction on Riverfolk trade post removal undermines a key counterplay recommendation, and several pre-p16 mechanical details are simplified or anachronistic.

## Verified claims

- "Tea (refreshes 5 items)" — correct for 1 tea on Refresh track: 2 extra flips + 3 base = 5 total refreshes per Birdsong `faction:vagabond$9.4.1`.
- "Stack 3+ warriors for Martial Law" — correct; Martial Law requires at least 3 warriors of another player in the target clearing `faction:woodland$8.4.2.2.1`.
- "Every warrior you kill generates a Conspiracy action" (counterplay section) — accurate in the intended direction: Cult warriors removed while *defending* go to Acolytes box per Revenge `faction:cult$10.2.3`. Applies only to defensive removal, not all contexts.
- Dominance requires 10 VP — correct `rule:3.3.1`.
- Eyrie's "ruling-tie advantage" for Dominance — Lords of the Forest gives rule on ties `faction:eyrie$7.2.2`; this aids Bird Dominance corner control.
- Commander "deals 1–2 hit swings" — Commander as attacker deals an extra hit `faction:eyrie$7.8.3`, so attacking Alliance bases with 1–2 effective hits before dice is defensible.
- Cult scores per suit with at least one garden on map — correct `faction:cult$10.5.3`.
- Riverfolk dividend scoring requires trade posts on the map — correct `faction:riverfolk$11.4.2`.
- "Kill warriors instead [of trade posts]" (underlying principle) — warriors require funds to replace (via Recruit spending one fund `faction:riverfolk$11.5.5`); the preference for warrior removal over post removal is itself sound even if the stated rationale is wrong (see Conflicts).

## Conflicts with the Law

- **"Do not destroy Trade Posts (costs 1 VP to you, free replacement for them)"** — directly contradicts `rule:11.2.5.1` (Trade Disruption). When a trade post is removed, it is removed from the game **permanently** — there is no replacement of any kind. Additionally the Riverfolk lose half their funds, rounded up. Destroying posts is actually one of the most effective counters to the Riverfolk, not a trap to avoid. The Key Takeaway bullet repeats this error: "destroying Trade Posts is a free replacement." Source is wrong on both the mechanical fact and the resulting strategic advice.

## Stale or outdated

- Source predates the Vagabond Infamy system. The current Infamy rule (`faction:vagabond$9.2.9.3.1`) scores 1 VP per piece of a Hostile faction removed in battle beyond the triggering warrior — a significant VP engine the source does not mention.
- Source predates later Lizard Cult rework (post-2019 printings changed how Outcast and Hated interact and how the Lost Souls pile feeds acolyte economy). The current Cult description (scoring via Score ritual, not a simpler per-garden VP) is accurate as presented in the Distilled section, but the phrase "score every turn" understates the dependence on hand cards and the once-per-suit-per-turn limit `faction:cult$10.5.3`.
- Covers base game and Riverfolk expansion only. All 2022–2025 factions (Lord of the Hundreds, Keepers in Iron, Lilypad Diaspora, Twilight Council, Knaves of the Deepwood) and Hirelings content are absent. Generalizations like "Marquise and Eyrie are natural armies" may not hold in all lineups that include the later high-aggression factions.
- Vagabond counterplay section says "making them retreat twice" is enough to set them back. The Vagabond pawn cannot be removed from the map `faction:vagabond$9.2.2`. "Retreat" here is informal (they take item damage and become less functional), but it may mislead new players who read "retreat" as movement.
- The source describes the Eyrie's "Despot + Commander" as the military path — the distinction between leader archetypes and the consequences of turmoil have been refined in later community discussion and in p16.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Dominance is the more viable path" for Marquise — the [Marquise profile](../../factions/marquise.md) takes the opposite position: dominance is evaluated only "when you're far behind and an opponent is close to 30 VP." This is a meaningful divergence between the 2019 source and the current project profile. Pass through as a period-appropriate judgment, not a rule contradiction.
- "Crafting in Rabbits: extra actions" — this is an informal shorthand for the fact that rabbit-suit crafted items (e.g., Command Warren) grant action-equivalent benefits. No Law violation, but not a precise mechanical statement.
- "Eyrie should turmoil at most once, intentionally" — defensible strategic principle; the [Eyrie profile](../../factions/eyrie.md) acknowledges deliberate turmoil as a legitimate tactical tool but flags it as risky.
- "Woodland Alliance: Keep supporter pile free of base-suit cards" — This is a reading of the base-destruction penalty (`faction:woodland$8.2.4`): losing a base purges matching supporters. Keeping those cards in hand rather than the supporter pile insulates you from the loss. Mechanically coherent but advanced advice that newer players may misread as "don't put base-suit cards in supporters" generally, which would harm early revolts.
- "Exponential factions win by default if unchecked" — The scoring curve framework predates expansions that complicate this taxonomy (Keepers in Iron and Twilight Council, for example, have non-exponential engines). Accurate for the six factions it covers.

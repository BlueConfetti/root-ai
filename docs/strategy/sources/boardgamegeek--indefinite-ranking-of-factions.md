# Distilled

## General

### Ranking Overview
Author ranks all 8 factions through Underworld. Opinions are explicitly personal ("this is strictly my own opinion"). Written assuming high-level game familiarity.

Note: The author initially described Corvid Snare as "locking everyone out of a territory" — corrected by a commenter (Jim Bauch) that Snare traps pieces *in* the clearing, not out of it. The corrected version: Snare traps enemy pieces in the clearing.

---

## Marquise de Cat

Placed in **S-Tier** ("I'd put the Marquise in the S-Tier"). Author values accessibility and relative simplicity. Core loop: generate wood, build buildings, score VP through building tracks. Can feel boring to experienced players but reliable for beginners. Risk of over-expanding — spreading too thin makes policing the whole map impossible.

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

The Decree system is described as a "programming minigame from hell." Author values the multiple leader paths (Charismatic for early troops, Despot/Commander for military, Builder for points). Downside is Turmoil loss risk and fragility to card draw variance.

---

## Woodland Alliance

Not ranked explicitly but discussed as a strong faction. Spreads Sympathy, organizes, revolts.

---

## Vagabond

Capable of multiple win paths. Strong once items are established.

---

## Lizard Cult

Strong engine but heavily reliant on suit matching. Spreading gardens across suits for flexibility is the recommended approach.

---

## Riverfolk Company

Flexible economic faction. Success depends on opponents being willing to trade and on timing the point burst correctly.

---

## Underground Duchy

Strong military faction. Author places high in ranking.

---

## Corvid Conspiracy

**Rules correction noted**: Author initially described Snare as preventing entry to a clearing; corrected by commenter — Snare traps pieces *inside* the clearing (cannot move out or be placed in, per the author's revised understanding). After correction, Snare is "still nice but much more manageable."

# Key Takeaways

- **Marquise is S-Tier per this author**: Accessible, reliable, rarely makes catastrophic mistakes; over-expansion is the main risk.
- **Corvid Snare traps pieces IN the clearing, not locks others out**: The author had this backwards initially — the correction is important for accurate counterplay assessment.
- **Eyrie is high-variance**: Multiple paths to success but Decree fragility makes skilled play essential.
- **Duchy is ranked high**: Strong military + consistent scoring.

## Verified claims

- "Snare traps enemy pieces in the clearing (cannot move out or be placed in)" — correct per `rule:13.7.2` ("enemy pieces cannot be placed in or moved from its clearing"). Both constraints named in the doc match the Law.
- Eyrie leaders named (Charismatic, Despot, Commander, Builder) with characterizations — all four leaders and their primary mechanical effects verified against `rule:7.8.1`–`rule:7.8.4`.
- Turmoil described as the Eyrie's core downside — confirmed; turmoil triggers when a Decree action cannot be fully resolved (`rule:7.7`).
- Cult "heavily reliant on suit matching" — verified; Score ritual requires matching suit, bird cards excluded (`rule:10.2.2`, `rule:10.5.3`).

## Stale or outdated

- Source predates the Marauder expansion (Sept 2022) and covers only 8 factions through Underworld — no Lord of the Hundreds, Keepers in Iron, Lilypad Diaspora, Twilight Council, or Knaves of the Deepwood content. Source Notes already flag this.
- Rankings reflect the 2023 metagame; balance perceptions may have shifted with the additions of 5 more factions across Marauder and Homeland expansions.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Marquise is S-Tier" — defensible judgment; consistent with the [Marquise profile](../../factions/marquise.md)'s characterization of the faction as highly accessible and reliable.
- "Corvid Snare is still nice but much more manageable" after the correction — defensible; the [Corvid profile](../../factions/corvid.md) describes Snare as an ongoing lockout that prevents movement out and placement in, an effect with genuine strategic value but less board-warping than a full entry blockade would be.
- "Duchy is ranked high, strong military + consistent scoring" — defensible; the [Duchy profile](../../factions/duchy.md) characterizes the faction as punctuated-burst scoring with strong parliamentary action economy.
- "Riverfolk success depends on opponents being willing to trade" — defensible; the [Riverfolk profile](../../factions/riverfolk.md) centers the service-buying mechanic as the faction's economic engine.

## Minor incompleteness (not an error)

The Snare correction says "cannot move out or be placed in" but does not clarify that enemies **can** move into a snarred clearing via a Move action (only placement into the clearing is prohibited, not movement into it). The Law says "placed in or moved from" (`rule:13.7.2`) — movement into a snarred clearing is unaffected. This is a nuance omitted by the source, not a factual error.

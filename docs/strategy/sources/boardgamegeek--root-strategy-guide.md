# Distilled

## General

### Combat Math
Attackers roll two dice (values 0–3) and take the higher result; defenders take the lower. Expected extra hits for attacker: 1.25. The attacker advantage is real but costs an action, making liberal combat a poor trade for action-scarce factions like Marquise. For action-rich factions (Riverfolk, Vagabond), the math favors more frequent fighting.

### Board Geography (Autumn Map)
Each corner has two adjacent clearings of the same suit, with a third of that suit two moves away — useful for Eyrie decree planning and Marquise workshop placement. Mouse clearings have the most building slots (8 uncovered) and are spread across several high-connectivity zones; a mouse Favor is correspondingly easier to set up and more destructive. The five-connection fox clearing in the south-west is the single most strategically valuable clearing on the board: it enables rapid reinforcement across the south and west. Two clearings with only two connections (one rabbit north, one fox south-west) make defensible satellite positions. The north-west and south-east corners are the farthest apart; other corner pairs are only three moves from each other.

### Cards and Crafting
Mouse suits offer the highest one-crafter value: over 20% of craftable cards need a single mouse crafter, and that one crafter nets over a quarter of all available crafting VP. Rabbit crafting contains the most total crafting VP and key upgrade cards (Command Warren, Cobbler, Better Burrow Bank) but requires two crafters. Fox crafting is mostly bird-suit cards and the best Vagabond items, so be cautious crafting those if a Vagabond is in play. Author's baseline: one mouse crafter plus two rabbit crafters covers most high-value options. With four crafters, adding one fox crafter reaches near-complete access including Royal Claim.

### Ambush Cards
All players: ambush cards are scarce (five in the game) and frequently misused. Ambushing a large army attacking lightly defended buildings rarely changes the outcome — the attacker just clears through anyway. Save ambushes for small raids where they can actually prevent the loss.

## Marquise de Cat

### Win Condition / Scoring Path
Stevens recommends dominance victories as the primary path for most games, citing that Cole (implicitly Cole Wehrle) achieved two-thirds of his Cat wins via dominance. Dominance avoids wasting limited actions on pure score-building and lets the Cats focus their military. Crafting victories are viable but more speculative; they depend on getting workshops early and drawing the right cards. Score-based victories require crafting efficiency and not allowing wood to stockpile.

### Opening Priorities
Marquise starts with three actions per turn — preserving action efficiency is the central constraint throughout the game. Turn 1 (if acting early in turn order): build a Sawmill, Overwork it immediately, then build either a second Recruiter or a Workshop depending on starting hand. If acting after the Alliance, evaluate whether early Alliance sympathy is a genuine threat before committing to a Workshop. Goal for early turns: get one Recruiter per turn while also establishing Workshops. Aim to reach three Recruiters (and thus three cards drawn per turn) as fast as possible, either via three Sawmills or two Sawmills plus two Overworks.

### Starting Location
Four corners evaluated:
- **North-East**: Easy rabbit crafting access but poor accessibility, isolation risk, vulnerability to Favor of the Mice and Alliance sympathy. Not recommended as default.
- **South-East**: Good mouse control potential but worst rabbit crafting corner; the opposite corner can contest rabbit clearings easily. If starting here, fortify three nearby mouse clearings and aim for mouse dominance.
- **South-West**: Best overall if the five-connection fox clearing can be held. Excellent accessibility and defensibility. Access to two rabbit clearings satisfies workshop needs. Only corner within two moves of every other corner, enabling all four dominance suits. Recommended as primary choice.
- **North-West**: Flexible, good rabbit access, but poor accessibility; similar weaknesses to north-east. Treat the five-connection fox clearing as the key pivot point.

Key criteria: (1) rabbit crafting (two workshop slots), (2) accessibility to defend key positions in one action, (3) defensibility of the Keep.

### Mid-game Pivots
Dominance path: secure military control, do not activate the dominance card immediately upon reaching strength — soften opponents first. Depleted opponents are much easier to hold three clearings against. Recruiters placed on target dominance clearings allow warrior replenishment if the first activation attempt fails. With five Recruiters and Field Hospitals, the Marquise can absorb eight to ten losses in a round and retake a contested clearing.

Score path: craft steadily, avoid large wood stockpiles (a wood pile is a tempting target, especially for a Despot Eyrie). Maintain enough military pressure on opponents to prevent someone else winning first.

### Threat Matchups
- **Eyrie**: The most direct opponent. Watch the Eyrie's Recruit decree suit; if they have only one or two Roosts recruiting a single suit, taking those Roosts forces turmoil. Do not aim for total Eyrie elimination — the Eyrie's presence helps suppress faster-scoring minor factions. Keep them down, not out.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Do not fight bases head-on — statistically you kill more than one warrior only 25% of the time. Overwhelm with warriors instead. Apply Martial Law whenever sympathy appears; blockade rather than battle at Alliance bases. If the Alliance is burning actions fighting a blockade, they cannot Organize.
- **Vagabond**: Attack early before the Vagabond accumulates swords. On a dominance path, consider offering coalition before playing the dominance card. Once hostile, flood Vagabond-adjacent clearings with your own warriors to slow their movement.
- **Lizard Cult**: Treat them as a high priority threat post-rework. Team with Eyrie and Alliance players to suppress Cultists. Discard cards liberally with Field Hospitals to manipulate the outcast suit. Keep Cultists locked on one suit's clearings; deny them the dominance card for that suit. Check the outcast suit before playing any dominance card.
- **Riverfolk**: Limit the table to two warrior purchases per turn from the Riverfolk. Kill Riverfolk warriors whenever more than one or two appear in a clearing — recruiting is expensive for them and their warriors on the board translate directly into actions.

### Card Priorities
Rabbit crafting is the top priority: Command Warren (free battle at start of Daylight), Cobbler (free move to rearrange warriors), Better Burrow Bank (extra card draw). All three directly offset the action constraint. Mouse secondary: Tax Collectors (warrior converts to card), Scouting Party (one warrior can clear undefended tokens). Fox tertiary: Codebreakers (scout for Favor and Ambush cards in hand). Armorers (fox) worthwhile only in the final stages before a dominance attempt — negating a high roll at the critical moment can be the difference.

Workshop build order recommendation: two rabbit then one mouse, or one mouse then two rabbit. A single rabbit workshop is not efficient enough to justify holding — get the second rabbit workshop quickly or skip rabbit temporarily. A fourth workshop (one mouse + two rabbit + one fox) covers nearly every useful crafting option including Royal Claim.

Bird cards: treat each one as a precious resource regardless of strategy path — they are additional actions and Field Hospital fodder simultaneously.

### Common Pitfalls
- Spending all three actions on combat against sub-optimal targets. Trading an action for one or two warriors is a net loss; attack undefended structures or perform key interventions only.
- Building for score rather than building for military control when a dominance win is the better path.
- Allowing wood to accumulate into a large pile — it becomes a VP windfall for anyone who plays a Favor card or manages a revolt near it.
- Activating a dominance card too early before opponents are weakened; three coordinated defenders can break through one clearing even from full strength.
- Forgetting Field Hospitals entirely (the updated rule lets one card save all warriors removed in a single action, making it a tactical option rather than a luxury).

## Eyrie Dynasties

### Win Condition / Scoring Path
Eyrie scores from Roosts, with VP scaling as Roosts accumulate. Goal is to get all or nearly all Roosts and warriors onto the board by round five, then shift into mid-game military control. Turmoil is not a catastrophe if planned for — turmoil on Build with few bird cards in the decree and most Roosts already out recovers quickly.

### Opening Priorities
Leader choice depends on starting hand:

- **Charismatic**: Ideal if starting hand has one card matching the home clearing's suit (for Move) and one matching two adjacent same-suit clearings (for Build). Can get all Roosts down by turn four before turmoiling deliberately. Charismatic falls off in the late game as its bonus becomes easy to replicate through normal recruit actions.
- **Builder**: Safe fallback if cards do not match Charismatic's requirements. Bird into Recruit and Move are low-risk; get a non-bird into Build to expand and craft early VP. Slower start, compensate with crafting.
- **Commander**: Best as a follow-up to Charismatic after Roosts are established. Just fight everyone and hold territory.
- **Despot**: Viable as a no-turmoil opener if fighting factions with many tokens down (e.g., Alliance). Alternatively, use Despot after Charismatic if all Roosts weren't placed in the first phase. Do not start as Despot solely for the bird-in-build, then immediately swap — not worth it.

### Decree Discipline
1. Recruit is deceptively dangerous: if an opponent takes out all Roosts in the suit being recruited, the Eyrie turmoils on Recruit, something they cannot prevent on their own turn. Always maintain backup Roosts in any recruited suit; never recruit in a suit with only one Roost.
2. Move is almost never the cause of turmoil — generally safe to load.
3. Always add two cards to the Decree each turn even when conservative; extra Move or Battle actions compound over time.
4. Conserve bird cards for critical moments rather than front-loading them into the Decree; non-bird cards make turmoil cheaper.
5. Turmoil is acceptable as long as it is planned and not the result of running out of warriors on Recruit.

### Mid-game Pivots
Commander phase: move around and suppress opponents threatening to overtake in score. Maintain the Cats as a natural mid-game ally against faster-scoring minor factions. Place another Build card when scoring drops below 4 VP per turn or when taking heavy losses. Despot phase: leave Roosts occasionally undefended to bait destruction (generates turmoil on opponent terms but fuels Despot ability). When turmoil arrives under Commander, swap to Despot; vice versa.

### Threat Matchups
- **Marquise de Cat**: Natural ally in mid-game; shared interest in suppressing Alliance and Vagabond. Attack Cats only once your roost-scoring is secured. Target Recruiters and the Keep to disrupt their engine; superior combat power (five possible hits) is the Eyrie's primary lever.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Commander with Brutal Tactics is very strong against Alliance bases (two free hits before rolling — source notes this nullifies Guerrilla War). But only attack bases with two or fewer Alliance warriors; a base with three-plus defenders has already been held long enough to not be worth the action.
- **Vagabond**: Roosts are soft targets for an item-laden Vagabond. Keep track of how many items the Vagabond has, particularly swords — that is the best proxy for their threat level.
- **Lizard Cult**: Do not recruit on non-bird cards if the Cult is in play with a strong position — Lizards can manipulate the outcast suit to turmoil Eyrie on Recruit. Gardens in Decree-required clearings form hard choke points; the Eyrie must resolve actions in order and cannot attack-move-attack in a single turn, making garden-blocked corridors extremely difficult to navigate.

### Card / Item Priorities
Crafting is card-draw dependent; priority upgrades: Brutal Tactics (pairs with Commander for two free hits), Tax Collectors (trims excess warriors before Recruit turmoil, converts warriors to cards), Armorers and Sappers (defends Roosts, reduces warrior dependency). Bird cards: do not over-invest in Decree. Source says "anything goes" for Eyrie crafting but not giving the Vagabond fox items is consistently worth noting.

### Common Pitfalls
- Over-loading Recruit in the Decree without maintaining backup Roosts in each recruited suit.
- Stockpiling bird cards in the Decree when non-bird cards could reduce turmoil cost.
- Attacking Lizard gardens only for the warriors — need to destroy the gardens themselves for the attack to matter.
- Neglecting the possibility of turmoil until it is forced rather than planned.

## Woodland Alliance

### Win Condition / Scoring Path
Alliance scores from Sympathy tokens (each placed token is worth approximately one card from opponents) and Bases (unlock Officers). Ideal endgame: stay at approximately 15 VP while accumulating cards and board position, then spike from 15 to 30 in a single dramatic turn. The "big turn" combines birdsong Sympathy placement, Daylight crafting off newly placed Sympathy, and Evening Organize into more Sympathy.

### Opening Priorities
Start with six cards: three in hand, three in Supporters. Before turn one, map out every possible combination to identify at least two viable Revolt suits. Revolt on turn two almost always. Very few cards justify delaying past turn two (a Favor card, Ambush, or key craft might). For first Base placement: choose a safe, central, not-isolated clearing. Avoid clearings adjacent to Cat or Eyrie starting positions, clearings with multiple enemy warriors or buildings, and the two low-connectivity clearings (two paths only) that can be bottlenecked easily.

Once the first Base is established, recruit to five Officers as fast as possible via additional Revolts or Train. Keep most Officers as base defenders; too many officers means too few warriors in the field.

### Mid-game Pivots
The "escort the payload" concept: with four Officers, you can move warriors two spaces, Organize one, and Recruit back at base — placing a heavily defended Sympathy in an important clearing that opponents will struggle to remove. Defender math: one warrior on Sympathy gives opponents only a 25% chance of destroying it in one action. Two warriors drops that to 6%. Armorers on Alliance makes the defender nearly invincible. From this position, Sympathy can be placed aggressively into high-value opponent clearings.

Constant goal: convert cards to points at the most efficient rate. Each card spent on Sympathy is approximately one VP. If an opponent spends an action to destroy a Sympathy token they have effectively paid the Sympathy's cost for you while you scored. Stay slightly behind the leader in raw VP so opponents do not perceive you as the most urgent threat.

### Threat Matchups
- **Marquise de Cat**: Main adversary due to sheer number of clearings and warriors. Aim to not be the Cats' top priority. Revolting adjacent to your own Base creates exit paths for spreading Sympathy. Target large Cat wood stockpiles with revolts — they translate to large VP swings.
- **Eyrie Dynasties**: The Eyrie's static Decree creates placement opportunities: put Sympathy wherever the Eyrie must move through, battle, or build. Force the Eyrie to donate cards through Outrage. Migrate away from the Eyrie as Commander grows stronger — they become too combat-capable to safely orbit. A Despot Eyrie eating Sympathy feeds you cards but also scores VP; manage greed carefully.
- **Vagabond**: No good direct counters. A Crossbow negates Guerrilla War. Let other factions handle the Vagabond. Do not craft fox items for the Vagabond; befriend them modestly (weak items via Aid) to keep them from using the Scoundrel's Scorched Earth on your bases.
- **Riverfolk**: Minimal interaction. Never buy Riverfolk services — they can withhold purchased warriors on the Riverfolk board, depriving you of Officers. Scatter warriors to suppress opportunistic Otter sympathy harassment.
- **Lizard Cult**: Play the outcast suit aggressively with bird cards. Sit on one Base as long as possible; prevent Sanctify by keeping your Base suit off the outcast. Revolts in Lizard-held clearings kill Lizard warriors without generating Acolytes — devastating their economy. Spreading Sympathy costs the Lizards disproportionate Acolytes to remove.

### Card / Item Priorities
- Armorers: the single highest-impact craft for Alliance — pushes Guerrilla War from strong to near-invincible.
- Better Burrow Bank and Tax Collectors: cards-for-cards, which compound faster than any other Alliance resource. Tax Collectors' warrior cost is trivially recovered by recruiting back in Evening.
- Scouting Party or Codebreakers: protect against Ambush cards that would otherwise flip the combat odds.
- For VP crafting: mouse and rabbit cards only. Avoid fox crafting — fox cards contain the best Vagabond items.

### Common Pitfalls
- Placing first Sympathy in a clearing that gets immediately cleared, losing the turn-two Revolt window.
- Spending cards on Sympathy in sub-optimal suits (cards going to Martial Law instead of scoring or positioning).
- Letting VP spike too high too early — experienced players will collectively focus the Alliance and prevent the big turn.
- Overloading on Officers at the expense of board defenders, leaving Bases with one warrior and inviting attacks.
- Buying Riverfolk services (warriors held on their board can be denied at a critical moment).

## Vagabond

### Win Condition / Scoring Path
Scoring comes from three sources, roughly in order of late-game efficiency: combat infamy (two points per hit with three swords, potentially six points per attack round), Aid (two points per card with maximum relationship, requires spending cards), and crafting items (VP plus tool value). Quests provide cards early, then VP. The Vagabond's scoring ceiling is very high but depends on the table not coordinating to eliminate items early.

### Opening Priorities
First goal regardless of Vagabond type: loot all four Ruins to find the Hammer and Sword(s). First four turns should systematically visit Ruins. Assess which faction has the easiest time attacking you and avoid their territory. Riverfolk in particular will attack a Vagabond ending their turn in a clearing with multiple Otter warriors — simply avoid those clearings. Keep track of Eyrie expansion direction and stay clear. Cats are busy enough in early turns to mostly ignore a Vagabond not directly in their warriors.

Vagabond types:
- **Thief**: Most balanced. Starts with a Sword, Tea. Steal is situationally useful; pair with Codebreakers.
- **Tinker**: Highest potential; starts with Hammer and discard-search ability. Can craft Favor cards, which no other Vagabond can. Vulnerable to early attack because no Swords; only worth choosing if opponents are unlikely to coordinate pressure.
- **Ranger**: Starts with Sword and Crossbow; strong early defense. Torch repairs three items. Go combat build; identify infamy target early.
- **Vagrant**: Leans into Infamy via ability; vulnerable start (no Swords beyond default). Instigate avoids self-hits but Infamy score from the ability should not be overvalued versus just getting swords faster.
- **Arbiter**: Starts with two Swords — skips the vulnerable early phase almost entirely. Source names this the consistently strongest Vagabond. Can start generating large infamy points very early while being hard to shut down.
- **Scoundrel**: Cool ability (Scorched Earth) but no starting Swords, making them a prime early target. Better as a coalition companion than a solo winner. Scorched Earth does not score infamy points.

### Mid-game Pivots
One ruin-sword reached: meaningfully less vulnerable. Two swords: requires coordinated force to dent you. Shift from pure item collection to active scoring. Aid factions early (turn spare items — swords, boots, bags, tea — into cards, then into points). Best Aid targets are factions that get the least out of non-bird cards (Cats, Eyrie), or factions you want not attacking you. Three swords: optimal combat scoring begins. Expect roughly six infamy points per turn from three attack actions. At this stage, combat outperforms both Aid and crafting in points-per-action.

### Threat Matchups
- **Marquise de Cat**: Safe Aid target early (they get less from non-bird cards). Keep Cats from becoming too weak — their suppression of the Alliance is beneficial. Late game, Cats have the most buildings and tokens to convert to combat VP.
- **Eyrie Dynasties**: Straightforward to assess — count their Roosts. Bird cards are deceptively safe to give them (Eyrie rarely needs many). They rarely craft, preferring items go to the Vagabond anyway.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Good crafter for items; dangerous Aid recipient (each card is approximately 1 VP for them, compounding fast). Crossbow hard-counters Guerrilla War. Sympathy tokens are good infamy targets. Monitor officer count, base count, and supporter cards — those are the Alliance's real scoring resources, not their current VP total.
- **Lizard Cult**: Good craft partner, dangerous Aid recipient (cards are both actions and VP for Lizards). Attack lightly defended Gardens for high VP rounds while suppressing Cult growth. Do not generate Acolytes for them by killing Lizard warriors in combat (note: crossbow kills do not generate Acolytes per source — author flags this as a quirk).
- **Riverfolk**: Capable of assembling large armies and attacking. Kill Riverfolk warriors proactively to deny VP and limit their action economy. They are also decent crafters — get items from them before attacking. Do not give them high-value cards.

### Common Pitfalls
- Neglecting ruins early — the Hammer and Sword set the pace for the rest of the game.
- Playing a no-Sword Vagabond (Tinker, Scoundrel, Vagrant) against aggressive opponents — the early attack window is very costly.
- Over-investing in Aid at the expense of combat once three swords are available.
- Feeding the Alliance or Lizards cards through Aid without accounting for their VP-per-card conversion rate.

## Lizard Cult

### Win Condition / Scoring Path
Scoring is via Ritual (discarding matching-suit cards for VP). Source emphasizes: do not save cards to chase three- or four-VP rituals at the expense of two-VP rituals. Two VP for one action and one card is tied with a maximally-aided Vagabond for the best conversion rate in the game — and the Lizards don't need to move between clearings. With two Gardens per suit, the Cult can reach four to six VP per turn quickly if card draw cooperates. The score potential on two clearings matches the Eyrie scoring on six Roosts.

### Opening Priorities
Ignore early scoring; focus first turns on locking down clearings to establish two Gardens of each suit. Claim territories early — Gardens can be made defensible later. Priority: reach four card draws as soon as possible. Choose first clearings conservatively, toward the edges of the map, for survivability. Once four (or at minimum three) card draws are secured, enter the mid-game.

### Mid-game Pivots
Acolytes: do not sacrifice warriors into opponent armies to generate Acolytes — just let them serve as territorial pieces. Exception: use Acolytes to throw the Eyrie into Recruit turmoil, or to destroy an Alliance Base laden with many Supporter cards — those are the only two high-value Conspiracy uses when the outcast is not hated.

Save Acolytes otherwise for a Hated Outcast turn. To enable this, cultivate relationships with factions that discard cards readily (Cats and Alliance especially). Coordinate a shared enemy: forcing that faction to discard into a suit you want as Hated/Outcast accomplishes territory control, opponent crippling, and alliance-building simultaneously.

Sanctify targets by quality: in the early-mid game, Workshops the Cats just built (intended to Overwork next turn), double-Sawmill clearings (removing one prevents using either's wood), and Recruiters defending key choke points or border clearings. Use Conversion when possible over Sanctify: converting warriors then attacking is more efficient per Acolyte, and captures territory rather than just destroying a building.

Score regularly — do not bank points in anticipation of perfect three- or four-VP turns. Defend Gardens to sustain card draws. Two VP per action per card is enough.

### Endgame Routes
Once approaching 20 VP, shift to reckless Sanctification for points, attack undefended structures, then discard to score as much as possible in one dramatic turn. Check if cards can be crafted before discarding for score — crafting and discarding in the same suit on one turn can be a large point swing.

Dominance speculation: Stevens raises the possibility of a Lizard dominance victory (Acolyte generation from defended clearings, difficulty of attacking Gardens) but has not tested it. The Cats' ability to weaken opponents before activating dominance is a key advantage the Lizards lack.

### Threat Matchups
- **Marquise de Cat**: Identify the Cats' bottlenecks before Sanctifying indiscriminately. Best targets: freshly built Workshops (Overwork denied), paired Sawmills (removing one strands both's wood), Recruiters in defensively critical clearings. Use gardens as choke points — box in and partition Cat movement. Watch for Cat dominance plans; coordinate outcast manipulation to make that suit hated before they play the card.
- **Eyrie**: Gardens form hard choke points the Eyrie cannot bypass in one turn due to linear Decree resolution. Recruiting on non-bird suits becomes risky for Eyrie when Cult is present; coordinate Sanctification with other players to force turmoil on Recruit. Building Gardens in clearings the Eyrie needs to build in can destabilize their Decree cascade.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Both factions have devastating mutual counters. The Alliance cannot be Sanctified, Converted, or impeded by Gardens — the Cult's usual toolkit does not apply. Craft Command Warren as top priority for a flexible out-of-turn attack option. Negotiate cautious coexistence: the Alliance can drain Acolytes through repeated Sympathy placements that are hard for the Cult to remove.
- **Vagabond**: Very limited interaction — the Vagabond is immune to Sanctify, Conversion, and garden choke points. Crusade or Command Warren attacks are costly and often unattractive. Best approach: open diplomatic relationship, try to be an Aid recipient rather than a combat target. The Vagabond's crossbow kills do not generate Acolytes (source treats this as a quirk of combat classification).
- **Riverfolk**: Ideal trading partner. Lizards have more spare warriors than any other faction and benefit most from extra cards (each extra card is an extra action). Coordinate with the Riverfolk to target mutual opponents; consider paying Mercenaries to take a clearing for a Garden placement. Rule note (Stevens correction): Mercenaries can only be used in Daylight and Evening — they cannot be purchased for Dominance card plays.

### Card / Item Priorities
Rabbit upgrades are the most impactful: Command Warren (flexible attack, essential vs. Alliance Sympathy), Cobbler (rearranges defense), Better Burrow Bank (sixth action per turn). Unlike Cats, Lizards acquire these incidentally rather than through deliberate workshop chains — just craft whatever you can when the outcast permits. Stand and Deliver is the one three-cost card Stevens suggests holding: the extra action every turn outweighs giving the trailing player one VP, provided the trailing player is correctly identified (not just lowest current score). Favor cards: acquire and hold, but do not reveal them too early and draw collective attention.

### Common Pitfalls
- Neglecting two-VP ritual actions in favor of waiting for perfect three- or four-VP turns.
- Over-using Acolytes on minor targets when a Hated Outcast opportunity could deliver much larger returns.
- Over-feeding the Riverfolk with too many card purchases (they benefit from your patronage more than you do from theirs in the long run).
- Misreading the Vagabond matchup as manageable — the Vagabond has multiple tools against the Cult (crossbow, infamy attacks) while the Cult has almost none against the Vagabond.

## Riverfolk Company

### Win Condition / Scoring Path
Stevens argues strongly against the "trading posts = score" mindset. Trading posts as a VP source cap out at 18 VP; building them early makes the Riverfolk appear threatening and reduces trade volume. Instead: save trading post scoring for the late game. Score the middle of the game through crafting and combat. "Committing" warriors (keeping them on the board as latent actions) is better than "spending" them (deploying them and reducing future action economy). The final turn: deploy remaining warriors to open clearings for trading posts, attack with your own warriors on the best target, craft whatever is available, then place more trading posts in cleared territory.

### Opening Priorities
Always maintain a full card stock and organize cards oldest-to-newest — replace the oldest cards whenever spare actions allow. Constantly remind the table what services are available and what opportunities their cards could unlock (Riverboats, Mercenaries, cards for purchase). Get early sales in the first half of the game before opponents become wary of funding you. Setting prices: never one warrior (negates Protectionism); keep prices at two or three for most services; charge four for exceptional cards (Favor cards are worth charging four for or crafting yourself).

Avoid placing warriors aggressively early — it makes opponents nervous and reduces purchases.

### Mid-game Pivots
Think of the Riverfolk as "shady mercenaries" rather than passive traders. The Otter action economy allows a small squad to act as a pseudo-Vagabond: highly mobile (aquatic nature), capable of reaching undefended structures across the map, with no territorial defense obligation. Combat targets: Alliance Sympathy (Alliance will not trade, and removing Sympathy is low-risk). Target whoever is leading in score. Seek patronage deals: convince a player to fund your attack on their mutual enemy, keeping leftover warriors as "change."

Dividends: avoid or minimize. They cost warriors (future actions) and are an unreliable scaling path since opponents become less willing to fund you as you lead.

Crafting: weave with combat. Get one Mouse trading post early for one-crafter access; add one Rabbit trading post for easy coin crafting. Fox trading posts give Brutal Tactics access but risk enabling the Vagabond — be cautious if a Vagabond is in play. Craft Armorers and Scouting Party first (keeps warriors alive, which keeps action economy intact). Brutal Tactics on the short list.

### Threat Matchups
- **Marquise de Cat**: Bird cards are high-value offerings. Rabbit crafting cards also in demand — charge more. Do not attack the Keep on turn one ("thinking like an Otter" means positioning warriors between Sawmills and charging for Mercenary access to bypass them). Late game, Cats have the most structures on the board — let them build, then score heavily off demolishing them.
- **Eyrie**: Remind them that purchasing cards before their turn can prevent a specific turmoil. Sell at setup before leader choice — your cards visible early give the Eyrie information for their decree planning.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Almost no trade. Recruit Otter warriors directly into Alliance Sympathy clearings rather than moving in to attack. Scouting Party doubly essential — the Alliance Ambush (Guerrilla War + regular Ambush card) is catastrophic for a squad combat action.
- **Vagabond**: Generally unwilling to trade. Strong infamy target — pay others to attack the Vagabond with Mercenaries, keep leftover warriors as change. Withhold Hammer cards (price up to four or discard) — denying the Vagabond a second Hammer slows their sword-crafting chain for most of a deck cycle. Poke the Vagabond even without being paid to do so: keeping them down is broadly beneficial and opponents should be aware the Vagabond's win rate is high.
- **Lizard Cult**: Best trading partner. Lizards have more spare warriors than anyone and benefit most from every service. Coordinate with Lizards to attack mutual threats; consider helping them take clearings for Gardens (within the Mercenary timing rule). In the endgame, attack all those Gardens you helped build — Lizards struggle to stop you.

### Common Pitfalls
- Racing to build trading posts for dividends early rather than saving post-scoring for the endgame burst.
- Letting someone buy a warrior for one — negates Protectionism and sets a bad precedent.
- Overspending warriors on combat or dividends in mid-game, eroding the action economy that makes the Riverfolk dangerous.
- Letting the Vagabond acquire a Hammer unchallenged — withholding it is a straightforward tempo move.

# Key Takeaways

- **General:** Attacker combat advantage averages 1.25 extra hits per battle; fighting is only worth it for action-rich factions. One mouse crafter plus two rabbit crafters covers the most impactful upgrades for most factions.
- **Marquise de Cat:** Dominance is the recommended primary win path (two-thirds of expert Cat victories). South-West corner is the preferred start if the five-connection fox clearing can be held. Action efficiency is the central constraint — never trade actions for marginal gains.
- **Eyrie Dynasties:** Never Recruit into a suit with only one Roost. Charismatic + deliberate early turmoil is the recommended opening when cards cooperate. Turmoil on your own terms beats turmoil forced by opponents.
- **Woodland Alliance:** Stay below the VP threshold that triggers collective suppression; plan a single large scoring turn from ~15 VP to 30. Armorers is the highest-impact craft in the game on the Alliance. Each card spent on Sympathy is approximately one VP — every opponent action to destroy a Sympathy token has "paid for itself."
- **Vagabond:** Arbiter is the consistently strongest Vagabond type (two starting Swords skip the vulnerable phase). Three Swords equals optimal scoring; combat at that stage outperforms Aid and crafting per action. Do not over-Aid the Alliance or Lizards — their card-to-VP conversion is too efficient.
- **Lizard Cult:** Two-VP ritual actions are as efficient as any conversion in the game; do not skip them chasing larger scoring turns. Save Acolytes for Hated Outcast turns or the two highest-value Conspiracy uses (Eyrie Recruit turmoil, Alliance Base destruction). Command Warren is the most important craft.
- **Riverfolk Company:** Do not rush trading posts for dividends — save the 18-post scoring for the endgame burst. Think of the faction as mobile mercenaries: combat and crafting score the middle game. Never let anyone pay one warrior for a service.

## Conflicts with the Law

- "one warrior on Sympathy gives opponents only a 25% chance of destroying it in one action. Two warriors drops that to 6%." — The probability math is reversed. With Guerrilla War (`faction:woodland$8.2.2`) the attacker takes the lower die result. With **one** defending warrior: P(lower die ≥ 1) = P(both dice ≥ 1) = (3/4)² ≈ 56%; dealing 1 hit removes the warrior, the sympathy is then defenseless (`rule:4.3.5.2`) adding a bonus hit, so the sympathy is also removed. The 25% figure actually corresponds to **two** warriors: P(lower die ≥ 2) = P(both dice ≥ 2) = (2/4)² = 25%. The source has the one-warrior and two-warrior figures switched. The strategic point (more warriors = harder) is correct, but the cited probabilities are transposed.

- "superior combat power (five possible hits) is the Eyrie's primary lever" — stated as the Eyrie's baseline threat in the Marquise section. Commander (`rule:7.8.3`) adds one extra hit as attacker; maximum dice roll is 3. Commander alone gives 4 max hits. Reaching 5 requires both Commander and Brutal Tactics simultaneously. This is achievable but not a baseline capability; presenting it without qualification overstates typical Eyrie offensive power.

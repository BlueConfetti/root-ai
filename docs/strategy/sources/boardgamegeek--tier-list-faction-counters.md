# Distilled

## General

### Community Win-Rate Rankings (by player count)
Data from a community spreadsheet. Rankings listed best to worst:

- **3 players**: Corvids > Woodland Alliance > Marquise > Eyrie > Lizard Cult > Underground Duchy > Vagabond > Riverfolk
- **4 players**: Vagabond > Woodland Alliance > Riverfolk > Corvids > Underground Duchy > Lizard Cult > Eyrie > Marquise
- **5 players**: Underground Duchy > Vagabond > Woodland Alliance > Riverfolk > Eyrie > Lizard Cult > Marquise > Corvids

### Key Velensk Observations
- **Win rates are player-count-sensitive**: Player count dramatically changes faction performance rankings.
- **Balance comes from politics, not faction stats**: "The balance of Root comes largely from the politics of aggression among the players as opposed to from the strength of the factions themselves."
- **Community consensus exists on limited points**: Duchy is notably strong. Certain Vagabond characters (Tinker, Harrier) are strong. Corvids are notoriously difficult to win with against an informed table.
- **Vagabond high win rate is political, not mechanical**: New players either craft for Vagabond when they shouldn't or fail to police them — both inflate their win rate. In a competitive setting, Vagabond is not overpowered.

### Velensk's Table Rankings (advanced setup)
Post-advanced setup:
**Duchy > Vagabond > Eyrie > Warlord > Cult > Riverfolk > Marquise > Keepers > Alliance > Corvids**

Notable caveats:
- Alliance is "far more likely to win on Autumn map."
- Corvids are "far more likely to win in 3-player without Marquise."
- Dangerous pairs that drag each other down: Alliance/Cult, Vagabond/Warlord, Keepers/Marquise.

## Lord of the Hundreds (Warlord)

### Vagabond-Warlord Poisonous Pairing
The Vagabond and Warlord are the most toxic faction pairing in the game — worse than Cult/Alliance.

**Root cause**: Both compete for ruin items. Vagabond can grab items instantly; Warlord must mob ruins and wait a full turn. Vagabond can easily deny every ruin to the Warlord by intercepting mobs after they're placed. Without ruin items, Warlord cannot reach Command 2+ or Prowess — permanently crippled.

**Warlord's three options against Vagabond**:
1. Claim a distant ruin before Vagabond reaches it (near-impossible if Vagabond refuses to allow it).
2. Mob two ruins simultaneously (Vagabond only has one torch) — logistically expensive and Vagabond can still contest.
3. Beat Vagabond up to deny movement — requires committing battle actions while still needing to mob a ruin simultaneously.

**Gentlemen's agreement**: Giving Vagabond 3 ruins and Warlord 1 is a practical truce, though even 1 wrong item (e.g., the only Hammer) can cripple the Vagabond anyway.

**Additional dynamics**:
- Vagabond creates clearings Warlord cannot oppress.
- Warlord has an unusually easy time spending battle actions against Vagabond (high warrior supply, and Moods like Relentless/Bitter/Vengeful/Stubborn improve cost-benefit).
- Seating fix: split Vagabond and Warlord in seating order (like two Vagabonds) to reduce their turn-order interaction.

## Keepers in Iron

### Complexity Assessment (Velensk)
"The most complex faction in the game to play." Contends with Eyrie for Velensk's personal favorite. Strong potential ceiling but faces:
- Serious political challenge: threatens nearly every faction, even non-leaders.
- Tricky scoring: many failure modes in the relic/waystation chain.
- Fewer opposing large-army factions = better Keeper performance.

## Vagabond

### Political Balance
"The Vagabond presents a political challenge that most players are either unaware of or unable to evaluate properly." Crafting for Vagabond when you shouldn't, or not hunting them when you should — both inflate their win rate in casual play. Against an informed table, they are not overpowered.

# Key Takeaways

- **Player count radically changes the tier list**: Faction rankings at 3p vs 5p are nearly inverse for several factions.
- **Vagabond-Warlord is the most toxic pairing**: Both compete for ruin items; their presence together forces both into suboptimal play that benefits neither.
- **Duchy is the only faction with near-universal consensus as "notably strong"**: Even competitive experts agree on this.
- **Vagabond is strong because of politics, not mechanics**: Uninformed tables craft for them and fail to police them — both are preventable.
- **Corvids need Marquise absent or low player count to win**: Most likely to win in 3p games without Marquise.
- **Alliance/Cult and Keepers/Marquise are drag pairs**: Their mutual suppression depresses both factions' win rates when paired.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 7/10
**Verdict:** Mostly accurate strategic analysis with one fabricated game element (a non-existent Warlord mood) and one overstated mechanical dependency that partially undermines the Vagabond-Warlord matchup section.

## Verified claims

- "Vagabond can grab items instantly; Warlord must mob ruins and wait a full turn" — mechanically correct. Vagabond uses Explore (`rule:9.5.3`) on their own turn; Warlord gets ruin items only through Raze (`rule:14.4.1`), which fires in Birdsong after mobs are placed via Incite in the previous Evening.
- "Vagabond creates clearings Warlord cannot oppress" — correct. The Vagabond pawn is a faction piece (Glossary: Faction Piece) and therefore an enemy piece of any faction not in coalition; its presence in a clearing blocks Oppress (`rule:14.6.2`).
- "Seating fix: split Vagabond and Warlord in seating order (like two Vagabonds) to reduce their turn-order interaction" — strategic judgment consistent with the general principle that turn order separates reactive players.
- "Stubborn mood: ignores first hit in warlord's clearing" — matches `rule:14.7.7`.
- "Relentless mood" listed as a Warlord mood — verified, `rule:14.7.5`.
- "Bitter mood" listed as a Warlord mood — verified, `rule:14.7.1`.
- Tinker and Harrier identified as strong Vagabond characters — consistent with the [vagabond profile](../../factions/vagabond.md) which marks Tinker as the strongest base-game character and Harrier as having exceptional mobility.
- "Duchy is notably strong" near-universal consensus claim — consistent with Velensk's table rankings and the project's faction profiles.

## Conflicts with the Law

- "Moods like Relentless/Bitter/Vengeful/Stubborn improve cost-benefit" — **"Vengeful" is not a Warlord mood**. The eight Warlord moods per `rule:14.7` are Bitter, Grandiose, Jubilant, Lavish, Relentless, Rowdy, Stubborn, and Wrathful. "Vengeful" does not appear anywhere in the Law. Source has fabricated a mood name.
- "Without ruin items, Warlord cannot reach Command 2+ or Prowess — permanently crippled" — overstated. The Warlord can gain items via Looters (`rule:14.2.5`, taking items from defeated defenders' Crafted Items boxes) and via crafting with Contempt for Trade (`rule:14.2.3`, hoarding the item into the Hoard). Ruins are the fastest early-game item source, but their denial does not permanently foreclose Command 2+ or Prowess. Source overstates the ruin dependency.

## Strategic judgment notes

- Win-rate rankings (3p/4p/5p) — community spreadsheet data, not tournament data. Presented as directional by the source; no mechanical verification possible. Pass through with the source's own caveat.
- "Balance of Root comes largely from politics of aggression among the players" — strategic philosophy claim. Consistent with broad community consensus; no Law-level verification applicable.
- "Corvids are far more likely to win in 3-player without Marquise" — judgment claim. The [corvid profile](../../factions/corvid.md) would be the appropriate cross-reference; this reviewer did not surface a contradiction there.
- "Keepers in Iron: most complex faction in the game to play" — opinion of Velensk. Not verifiable against the Law; presented as individual assessment, not fact.
- "Alliance is far more likely to win on Autumn map" — map-specific judgment. Source predates any Homeland (2024) map content and covers only through Marauder expansion maps.

# Distilled

## Marquise de Cat

### Opening
Consolidate early: Build Sawmill, then two moves to abandon distant positions and create defensible clusters. The "Great Wall" strategy masses 4–5 warriors per clearing adjacent to the keep before expanding gradually.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Steady VP accumulation through building and crafting — not burst scoring. Slowing opponents matters as much as own point generation.

### Threat Matchups
- **Woodland Alliance**: Use martial law aggressively; destroy undefended sympathy tokens.
- **Eyrie**: Force turmoils; destroy roosts opportunistically; avoid large bird groups.
- **Vagabond**: Avoid crafting Tea; wound early if possible.
- **Riverfolk**: Purchase blue cards strategically while watching for mercenary counter-threats.
- **Lizard Cult**: Outscore them; destroy gardens only when convenient.

### Crafting Priorities
Sappers, Armorers, Cobbler (movement), Command Warren (free opening battles).

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

### Opening
Save blue cards for Battle and Build slots — they provide more flexibility. Color cards work for Recruit and Move. Avoid isolated roosts vulnerable to attack.

### Leader Selection
- **Despot**: Versatile across all phases; good if lacking blue cards (viziers assist).
- **Commander**: Least favored; only valuable mid-to-late for aggressive play.
- **Charismatic**: Strong early for rapid deployment; avoid over-recruiting.
- **Builder**: Situational late-game pick with high-point crafted cards.

### Crafting Priorities
Tax Collector (synergizes with Charismatic), Scouting Party (protects against ambush turmoils), Favor cards (withhold then deploy late).

### Threat Matchups
- **Marquise**: Maintain peace early; both benefit from containing other threats.
- **Lizard Cult**: Removing gardens takes priority since they restrict movement.
- **Vagabond**: Attack proactively despite minimal crafting reward; prevent escalation.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Blockade rather than push through sympathy; eliminate tokens promptly.
- **Riverfolk**: Establish mutually beneficial relationship; their services counter turmoil risk.

---

## Woodland Alliance

### Opening
Place 3 sympathy tokens in uncontested areas or Cat-adjacent areas without buildings. Load the entire starting hand into supporters unless holding an Ambush card or exceptional craft card.

### Phase Structure
- **Phase I (no bases)**: Spread sympathy for card advantage; monitor Eyrie for revolt opportunities.
- **Phase II (with bases)**: Choose among Rebel Base (officers + defense), Support of the People (2 officers, Move + Organize to spread fast), or The Empire Strikes Back (defensive against focused opponents).

### Threat Matchups
Multi-base Alliance players become primary targets — require defensive positioning.

### Crafting Priorities
Card-generation items; Armorers increase base survivability.

---

## Riverfolk Company

### Economics
Price Hand Cards at 3 coins for strong opening hands (Blue/Ambush); otherwise 2 coins. Eyrie is your biggest customer. Price Riverboats at 1 coin normally; raise when Eyrie needs river movement as their only option. Deploy Mercenaries where sides are roughly equal for maximum impact.

### Psychology
Never appear threatening. Stay slightly below the leader's point total. Surge around 20 points with accumulated funds. Reward early traders with immediate troop deployment; blockade hostile players.

### Crafting Priorities
Sappers (defensive prep), Armorers (craft immediately), Cobbler (positioning efficiency).

---

## General

### Board-State Reading
Root differs fundamentally from traditional wargames — piece count does not equal strength. A Woodland Alliance with 2 warriors, 4 sympathy, and 1 base threatens revolt in 3 clearings: a deceptively dominant position. Always evaluate each faction's unique mechanics, not raw piece totals.

### Dominance Victories
Watch for opponents concentrating forces in specific clearing types — it signals dominance-victory intent. Winter board configurations can make dominance wins more achievable than expected. The Marquise is well-positioned for dominance if they draw a matching clearing card early.

# Key Takeaways

- **Marquise**: Consolidate at keep with Great Wall before expanding; steady building beats burst scoring.
- **Eyrie**: Blue cards belong in Battle/Build slots; Despot is default safe leader; Charismatic is early-game aggressive pick.
- **Eyrie**: Zero wins after turmoil is corroborated by Make Craft Game tournament data.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Full starting hand into supporters (except Ambush); 3 opening sympathy tokens is optimal.
- **Riverfolk**: Price at 3 for blue/ambush, 2 otherwise; stay near leader VP total until final surge.
- **All factions**: Piece count is a misleading heuristic; evaluate faction mechanics, not army size.

## Stale or outdated

- Source is 2018-2019. Vagabond infamy rules (introduced in Riverfolk/Underworld expansion printings and formalized in later Law) substantially change Vagabond threat dynamics. The advice "avoid crafting Tea; wound early if possible" was valid under base-game Vagabond rules but the infamy system changes hostility mechanics.
- No Underworld, Marauder, or Homeland content — base-game-only scope means all "multi-faction" and general advice reflects a narrower player count / faction mix than modern games typically see.
- The source notes are absent on Lizard Cult strategy (Vagabond and Cult articles were never published). The Eyrie section mentions Cult threat briefly; see next section.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Lizard Cult: Removing gardens takes priority since they restrict movement" — imprecise. Gardens do not restrict movement; Cult's Pilgrims ability (`faction:cult$10.2.4`) overrides Lords of the Forest, preventing the Eyrie from ruling those clearings for Build Decree actions. The practical concern is real (Eyrie loses Build sites) but "restrict movement" is technically incorrect. The [Eyrie profile](../../factions/eyrie.md) correctly frames this as a rule interaction, not a movement restriction.
- "Eyrie: Zero wins after turmoil is corroborated by Make Craft Game tournament data" — external empirical claim from a third-party source (MCG). This is unverifiable from the Law and reflects 2018-era tournament data. Treat as period observation, not a current rule or up-to-date statistical claim. The Law does not prevent winning after turmoil; the VP penalty and momentum loss make it statistically difficult.
- "Woodland Alliance: 3 opening sympathy tokens is optimal" — the Key Takeaways phrase this as a discrete turn-1 target, but reaching three sympathy in turn 1 requires more supporters than the initial three (first token costs 1, second costs 1 in early positions, third may cost 2). This is realistically a turns-1-through-2 goal, not a single-turn target. The [Woodland Alliance profile](../../factions/woodland.md) frames this correctly as a multi-turn opening.
- "Commander: Least favored; only valuable mid-to-late for aggressive play" — consistent with the [Eyrie profile](../../factions/eyrie.md), which labels Commander "the most situational starting choice." Judgment, not Law.
- "Eyrie is your biggest customer" (Riverfolk section) — defensible strategic claim; Eyrie frequently needs river access and hand cards to satisfy the Decree, making them a natural Riverfolk client. The [Riverfolk profile](../../factions/riverfolk.md) agrees the Eyrie is among the most reliable buyers.
- "Surge around 20 points with accumulated funds" (Riverfolk) — the specific 20-VP threshold is a judgment call, consistent with the Riverfolk profile's endgame guidance around burst post-placement near 20 VP.

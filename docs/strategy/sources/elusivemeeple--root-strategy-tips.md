# Distilled

## Marquise de Cat

### Dominance Path
When holding a dominance card, mass forces along key clearings you can defend throughout entire turns. Territorial losses between strongholds are acceptable if the designated points remain controlled.

### Building Strategy
Balanced building across the map provides superior reactive flexibility versus aggressive early expansion. Defensive posture enables continuous rebuilding and countering opponent aggression.

### General
All factions should establish board presence early — allowing Cats or Birds to stockpile armies unchecked risks a dominant position that is hard to dislodge.

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

### Leader: Charismatic
Charismatic offers the most efficient early recruiting: 2-for-1 value on recruitment to home territory. Compounds in early game before additional reinforcement cards become necessary.

### Bird Cards
Require careful placement decisions each turn. Recruit strategically and move from reinforced locations to keep pre-programmed actions manageable and preserve cards for battles and building.

---

## Woodland Alliance

### Sympathy Placement
Spread sympathy where the Eyrie must move, limiting their movement without requiring military confrontation.

### Base Priority
Prioritize establishing bases to gain additional cards — greater strategic impact than focusing on military presence.

---

## Vagabond

### Item Economy
Target items from caves to acquire cards, then use those cards for additional item collection. Card-generating items are most valuable early due to this compounding effect.

### Healing Discipline
Avoid frequent healing — endure damage until severely wounded. Healing too often signals vulnerability and invites attack. Stay near skirmishes between other factions rather than confronting large armies.

---

## General

### Universal Early Advice
Establish board presence from the start across all factions. Securing crafting capabilities early prevents military success from being undermined by opponent trap cards.

# Key Takeaways

- **Marquise**: Balanced building across the map > aggressive early expansion; dominance requires defending key clearings per turn.
- **Eyrie**: Charismatic leader is the efficient early pick; manage bird cards carefully to avoid over-programming.
- **Woodland Alliance**: Place sympathy in Eyrie's required movement paths; base establishment trumps military presence.
- **Vagabond**: Card-generating items first; heal rarely — stay healthy enough to act but avoid telegraphing vulnerability.
- **All factions**: Act early; letting heavy factions stockpile armies unchecked is the most common strategic error.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 6/10
**Verdict:** Broad strategic instincts are mostly sound for beginners, but several faction-specific claims contain meaningful mechanical imprecision and one piece of Vagabond advice directly contradicts the project's faction profile.

## Conflicts with the Law

- "Charismatic offers the most efficient early recruiting: 2-for-1 value on recruitment to home territory." The Charismatic Recruit action (`rule:7.8.2`) places warriors in any clearing with a roost whose suit matches the Decree card suit, not specifically the home clearing. "Home territory" is inaccurate.

- "Spread sympathy where the Eyrie must move, limiting their movement without requiring military confrontation." Sympathy does not restrict movement. Per `rule:8.2.6`, moving warriors into a sympathetic clearing triggers Outrage — the opponent pays a card to the Supporters stack. Movement is not prevented or limited; only a card cost is imposed. Framing this as "limiting movement" misrepresents the mechanic.

## Stale or outdated

- Source published May 2019 (base game only). Predates Riverfolk Expansion content in full and all Underworld, Marauder, and Homeland content. Generalizations about "all factions" apply only to the base-game four.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Avoid frequent healing — endure damage until severely wounded." The [Vagabond profile](../../factions/vagabond.md) lists "Neglecting Repair" as an explicit common pitfall and notes that "going into battle with a damaged item pool narrows your combat ceiling." The source's advice directly conflicts with the project's vetted guidance. The source's framing that healing "signals vulnerability" has no mechanical basis in the Law — it is a table-dynamics claim with no rule support. Flag as unreliable.

- "Balanced building across the map provides superior reactive flexibility versus aggressive early expansion." The [Marquise profile](../../factions/marquise.md) states that "spreading builds evenly across all three types... is typically suboptimal — the back of each track is where the points concentrate." The source's "balanced building" framing is in mild tension with this, though it could be read as geographic spread rather than track distribution. Pass through with caveat that the profile's guidance on focused building is more precise.

- "Target items from caves to acquire cards, then use those cards for additional item collection." "Caves" is not a game term — the correct term is **ruins** (`rule:9.3.4`, setup step). The mechanism described also conflates Explore (gaining items, not cards) with Aid (giving cards to take items from Crafted Items boxes). Strategically directional but terminologically and mechanically imprecise.

- "Securing crafting capabilities early prevents military success from being undermined by opponent trap cards." "Trap cards" is non-standard terminology; likely referring to Ambush cards. Strategically sound but uses non-game-language.

- The Vagabond "Item Economy" claim about "card-generating items" being most valuable early aligns with the [Vagabond profile](../../factions/vagabond.md), which rates `item:coin` and `item:tea` as top priorities. This specific sub-claim is well-supported.

- Charismatic as the "most efficient early recruiting" leader is broadly consistent with the [Eyrie profile](../../factions/eyrie.md), which marks Charismatic as a safer opening choice alongside Despot. Not disputed, though the profile gives Despot equal billing for beginners.

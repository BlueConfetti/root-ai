# Distilled

## Lizard Cult

### Win Condition
Dual requirement: gardens on board AND matching suit cards in hand to score. "Slow and steady" approach — scoring every round is mandatory; delaying the first scoring round is a structural disadvantage.

### Garden Strategies

**Going Wide** (recommended default): Distribute gardens across all three suits (target 2 per suit). Yields 6 potential VP when all suit symbols are revealed, with maximum flexibility in hand management. The preferred approach for most board states.

**Going Tall** (niche): Concentrate all 5 gardens in one suit, yielding 4 VP per card spent. High variance — deck may not provide the necessary suit cards when needed.

**Clumping vs. Spreading**: Clumping (2 gardens per clearing) demands fewer controlled clearings but creates vulnerability — opponents can remove both gardens at low cost, triggering two Fear of the Faithful discards. Spreading reduces catastrophic losses but demands more clearing control.

### Key Mechanics

**Revenge**: Warriors that die go to acolytes box, not supply. This rewards getting attacked — losing warriors in battle is a net positive for action economy.

**Hatred of Birds**: Bird cards are weak for lizards. Suited cards provide multiple action options vs. bird cards' limited use.

**Pilgrims**: Gardens grant automatic clearing control regardless of warrior count, but this traps opposing factions — forcing combat as their only escape, which feeds your acolyte pool.

**Dominance Card Swapping**: Trade hand cards for matching-suit dominance cards to maintain consistent suit access while building lost souls composition.

### Turn Structure

**Birdsong — Adjust Outcast**: Most common suit in lost souls (previous round's discards) determines the outcast suit. If outcast repeats, it becomes "hated" (reduced conspiracy costs). This dictates conspiracy and crafting clearings for the turn.

**Crusade** (2 acolytes, 1 if hated): Battle in outcast clearings or move from them before battling. Primary movement mechanism. Serves both offense (protecting gardens) and defense (denying opponent points).

**Converting** (acolyte cost): Remove opponent warrior, replace with lizard from supply. Creates scoring opportunities and disrupts opponent positioning.

**Sanctify** (3 acolytes, 2 if hated): Transform buildings into gardens without requiring warrior presence. High risk — undefended gardens invite removal and trigger Fear of the Faithful.

**Daylight — Score**: Discard a card to score all matching-suit gardens; card enters lost souls. Score once per suit per turn. Consistent scoring > hoarding cards for uncertain crafting.

**Evening — Craft**: Only gardens matching outcast suit support crafting. Maintain minimum hand sizes (don't drop below 5 cards post-draw). Preserve scoring capability for next round.

**Acolyte Management**: Spend opportunistically rather than hoarding. Accumulating large reserves risks missing key moments and wastes actions if opportunities never materialize.

### Faction Matchups

- **Marquise de Cat**: Cats rarely prioritize lizards (action scarcity). Strategic recruitment within cat supply lines creates minor annoyances that invite battles without threatening gardens.
- **Eyrie Dynasties**: Provide "safety battles" — well-positioned lizard placement lets Eyrie fulfill Battle decree slots without threatening gardens. Maintains positive table relations.
- **Woodland Alliance**: The "smoking gun" relationship — WA movement taxation and revolts are challenging, but existing revolts create safe zones as remaining clearings resist further incursion.
- **Vagabond**: Prioritize crafting during vagabond presence for synergistic loops: play cards for actions, craft for items/points, receive vagabond aid restocking hand.
- **Riverfolk Company**: Purchase cards to expand action capacity — target scoring actions unavailable otherwise or high-value crafts that deny points to opponents.
- **Underground Duchy**: Convert lone duchy warriors (especially those supporting lord advancement) to force action expenditure and disrupt duchy momentum.
- **Corvid Conspiracy**: Converting corvid warriors prevents plot completion; limited direct interaction otherwise.
- **Lord of the Hundreds**: Warlord despises lizard presence. Recruit into warlord-controlled clearings to force combat responses while generating reliable acolytes.
- **Keepers in Iron**: Spread gardens across multiple clearings to maximize board control denial, forcing keepers toward contested routes or lengthy detours.

### Opening Examples (Autumn Map)

- **Mouse-Heavy Hand**: Top-right double-slot clearing — isolation with adjacent expansion opportunities.
- **Bunny-Heavy Hand**: Top bunny clearing — superior crafting and VP potential; higher visibility.
- **Fox-Heavy Hand**: Base-map fox double-slot clearing — turn-one scoring possible; fox clearings face less competition.
- **Bird-Heavy Hand**: Default to top-right mouse clearing — flexible access to multiple suits. Bird-heavy starting hands are generally poor for lizards.

# Key Takeaways

- **Dual requirement is non-negotiable**: Without both gardens on board AND matching cards in hand, scoring stops entirely. Manage both simultaneously.
- **Score every round**: Missing round-one scoring is a structural disadvantage that compounds. Prioritize the first score above all other actions.
- **Going Wide is default**: 2 gardens per suit gives flexibility and 6 potential VP when revealed. Going Tall is only viable in specific hand/board configurations.
- **Revenge rewards passive defense**: Let opponents attack your warriors — each combat death generates an acolyte. Don't avoid fights.
- **Spread gardens, not clumped**: Two gardens per clearing doubles the Removal cost to your opponent and halves the catastrophic discard punishment.
- **Opportunistic acolyte spending beats hoarding**: Large acolyte reserves are wasted potential — spend them on timely Crusades and Converts.
- **Outcast suit dictates everything**: Build your hand and board plans around what the outcast suit will be, not what you'd ideally prefer.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 6/10
**Verdict:** Strategically sound on garden width and acolyte management, but the Score ritual is mechanically misrepresented as "score all matching-suit gardens" rather than a single track-value VP award, and three secondary omissions (Revenge's defensive-only scope, Crusade's optional post-move battle, Outcast tie-breaking) reduce reliability as a rules reference.

## Conflicts with the Law

- "Discard a card to score all matching-suit gardens" (`# Key Mechanics` > Daylight — Score) contradicts `rule:10.5.3`. The Score ritual awards the VP printed above the rightmost empty Gardens space for the matching suit — a single fixed amount — not VP per garden on the map. Garden count on the map determines track position (and therefore the VP value), but each Score ritual pays once per suit, not once per garden.
- "Crusade (2 acolytes, 1 if hated): Battle in outcast clearings or move from them before battling" implies battle is required after movement. `rule:10.4.3` (Crusade) says "and then, **if you wish**, initiate a battle in the destination clearing" — the battle is optional.
- "Warriors that die go to acolytes box" (`# Key Mechanics` > Revenge) omits the defensive restriction. `rule:10.2.3` states the trigger is removal "while **defending** in battle." Warriors removed when the Cult is the attacker return to supply normally. The Key Takeaways phrasing ("let opponents attack your warriors") implies the correct context, but the Distilled description is incomplete.

## Stale or outdated

- No staleness issues for the factions covered. The source notes absence of Homeland content (Diaspora, Council, Knaves), and the Marauder-era matchups (Lord of the Hundreds, Keepers in Iron) are included. All p16 Cult mechanics are current.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Going Wide (recommended default): 2 per suit, 6 potential VP" — defensible. The [cult profile](../../factions/cult.md) scoring engine section reaches the same conclusion for practical garden spread.
- "Going Tall (niche): 5 gardens in one suit yields 4 VP per card" — aligns with the cult profile footnote on track values ("five gardens in a single suit can yield 4 VP per Score"). Treat as community-reported since exact track values are on the physical board, not in the Law.
- "Outcast tie-breaking" is not mentioned anywhere in the source. The Law (`rule:10.4.1`, Adjust Outcast) has an explicit tie rule: if no single suit holds the most non-bird cards, the marker stays on its current suit and flips to Hated. Omitting this creates a gap for new players who may not know how to resolve tied piles.
- Faction matchup advice is strategic judgment; broadly consistent with the [cult profile](../../factions/cult.md) Threat Factions section. No Law contradictions in matchup content.

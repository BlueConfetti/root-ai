# Distilled

## General

### Win Rates (Plus-One Draft, ~60 games)

| Faction | Win Rate |
|---|---|
| Riverfolk Company | 44% |
| Vagabond | 43% |
| Woodland Alliance | 31% |
| Lizard Cult | 27% |
| Eyrie Dynasties | 27% |
| Underground Duchy | 25% |
| Marquise de Cat | 21% |
| Corvid Conspiracy | 7% |

### Faction Selection Rates
- Vagabond: 100% (never declined)
- Eyrie Dynasties: 91%
- Riverfolk Company: 86%
- Woodland Alliance: 81%
- Underground Duchy: 78%
- Corvid Conspiracy: 60% (least chosen)

### Map and Deck Preferences
- Preferred deck: Exiles and Partisans over Standard
- Preferred map: Autumn (Winter as close alternative)

### VP Tracking Notes
Special conditions tracked: coalition usage marked as -1, dominance cards as -2. Data broken down by sitting position and faction performance.

# Key Takeaways

- **Riverfolk (44%) and Vagabond (43%) are the strongest tournament factions** in plus-one draft — significantly above other factions.
- **Corvid Conspiracy at 7% win rate** is the weakest tournament performer by a large margin; also least selected (60%).
- **Vagabond was never declined** — universally regarded as worth drafting in every game.
- **Eyrie at 27%** is middle-tier despite being the second most-selected faction (91%) — high pick rate does not correlate to high win rate.
- **Exiles and Partisans deck is preferred** over Standard in competitive play.
- **Autumn map is the dominant competitive map** choice.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Riverfolk (44%) and Vagabond (43%) are the strongest tournament factions" — valid interpretation of the reported data for this format and period; consistent with both the [Riverfolk profile](../../factions/riverfolk.md) characterizing it as high-complexity and commercially potent, and the [Vagabond profile](../../factions/vagabond.md) describing steady + burst VP generation.
- "Eyrie at 27% despite 91% pick rate" — directionally plausible; the [Eyrie profile](../../factions/eyrie.md) notes the faction can run away with the board but is vulnerable to turmoil during the early window, which may explain gap between desirability and win rate.
- "Corvid Conspiracy at 7% win rate, also least selected" — consistent with the [Corvid profile](../../factions/corvid.md) describing the faction as slow to build momentum and reliant on a burst that opponents can disrupt. Directionally defensible.

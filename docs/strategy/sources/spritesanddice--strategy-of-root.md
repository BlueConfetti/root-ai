# Distilled

## Marquise de Cat

### Win Condition
Score consistently through building and holding clearings. Marquise is the "main antagonist after setup" — expect early pressure from all sides.

### Opening
Stack warriors in key clearings early. Abandon outer territory to establish defensible clusters. Prioritize protecting Lumber Mills above all other structures.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Control roughly half to one-third of the map rather than spreading thin. Choke woodland paths through garrison placement rather than aggressive expansion. Multiple recruiting posts enable efficient troop concentration.

### Threat Matchups
- **Woodland Alliance**: Suppress sympathy actively to prevent revolts in critical clearings.

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

### Win Condition
Restore roosts and fulfill the decree. Potentially the most powerful military if the decree is managed carefully.

### Opening
Slot two cards in the decree on turn one. Prioritize bird cards in the "Build" slot — they provide the most flexibility for roost placement.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Build at least one roost per turn to accelerate scoring. Manage hand carefully — depleted hands invite turmoil. Crafting provides card draw even if VP-inefficient. Losing clearings and roosts is acceptable when it prevents turmoil.

### Pitfalls
"Crashes and burns if you cannot plan ahead." Highly dependent on card draw. The faction need not win every engagement — fulfilling decree orders matters more.

---

## Woodland Alliance

### Win Condition
Spread sympathy, revolts, establish bases. Robin Hood faction that gains power when opposed.

### Opening
Place sympathy in non-garrisoned central clearings. Load the supporter deck early. Preserve bird cards for suit pairs enabling revolts rather than spending them on sympathy.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Aim for 4–5 officers by endgame (3 is insufficient, excess limits warriors available). Use Organize to build hidden influence. Threat of revolt often deters expansion as effectively as actual revolts.

---

## Vagabond

### Win Condition
Clear ruins, fight, complete quests, aid factions. Two paths: aggressive hostile for destruction points, or pacifist building to reputation 3 for aide-stacking.

### Opening
Clear ruins early. Seek crafting aids to translate equipment into additional actions.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Starting equipment significantly shapes strategy. Wolf vagabond achieves higher equipment limits for specific items vs. Beaver. Coalition victories remain possible in 4+ player games.

---

## Riverfolk Company

### Win Condition
Trading post placement plus unspent fund dividends. Early pricing strategy is critical — reasonable rates accelerate engine development.

### Opening
Price cards competitively. Eyrie and Lizard Cult are primary early customers.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Build trading posts by positioning warriors in target clearings. Late-game card denial through elevated pricing. Craft A Better Burrow Bank and Tax Collector for fund-less draw. Avoid single-turn point bursts that invite collective embargoes.

---

## Lizard Cult

### Win Condition
Score by discarding cards matching suit of gardens on board. Dual requirement: gardens on board AND matching cards in hand.

### Opening
Target clearings with multiple building slots for maximum draw-symbol accumulation.

### Mid-Game / Engine
Early clearings with multiple slots accelerate draw. Trade with Riverfolk for card draw. Achieving "hated" status requires strategic outcast suit control. Recent rules changes improved faction viability significantly. Well-positioned for dominance victories through widespread garden networks.

---

## General

### Table Politics
- Monitor second-place players, not just the leader, to avoid enabling runner-up victories via leader-bashing.
- Confront the Vagabond before late-game acceleration becomes unstoppable.
- Prioritize card draw across all factions — cards represent fundamental power.
- Avoid traditional wargame overextension; selective territorial control beats map domination.
- Leverage table talk and alliance-building — Root's political dimensions reward negotiation.

# Key Takeaways

- **Marquise**: Garrison half the map defensively; protect Lumber Mills; choke paths rather than fighting everywhere.
- **Eyrie**: Bird cards in Build slot; manage hand size above all; losing battles is fine if decree is fulfilled.
- **Woodland**: 4–5 officers is the sweet spot; load supporters early; threat of revolt is as powerful as actual revolts.
- **Vagabond**: Ruins first, then crafting aids; equipment choice defines playstyle; coalition is a viable path.
- **Riverfolk**: Price competitively early; avoid point bursts that provoke embargoes; craft Tax Collector and Bank.
- **Lizard Cult**: Multiple-slot clearings for opener; card draw is the engine; dominance victory is underrated path.
- **All factions**: Card draw is the universal power multiplier; political awareness trumps pure military skill.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 6/10
**Verdict:** Broadly sound as a 2018 beginner overview, but contains naming errors, one mechanically incorrect revolt claim, a misleading Cult scoring description, and stale Vagabond terminology that predates p16.

## Verified claims

- Eyrie "Crafting provides card draw even if VP-inefficient" — correct; Disdain for Trade caps crafting VP at 1 per item (`faction:eyrie$7.2.3`).
- Eyrie "Losing clearings and roosts is acceptable when it prevents turmoil" — defensible; turmoil costs 1 VP per bird card in the Decree plus resets momentum (`faction:eyrie$7.7.1`).
- Riverfolk "Trading post placement plus unspent fund dividends" — correct two-engine description; dividends score 1 VP per 2 warriors in Funds box when any post is on the map (`faction:riverfolk$11.4.2`).
- Vagabond "Coalition victories remain possible in 4+ player games" — correct (`faction:vagabond$9.2.8`, `rule:3.3.1`).
- Woodland Alliance "Spread sympathy in non-garrisoned central clearings" — mechanically correct; Martial Law adds cost for clearings with 3+ enemy warriors (`faction:woodland$8.4.2.2.1`).
- Woodland Alliance "Revolt often deters expansion as effectively as actual revolts" — strategic judgment consistent with the [Woodland Alliance profile](../../factions/woodland.md).

## Conflicts with the Law

- Marquise section uses "Lumber Mills" throughout; the correct game term is Sawmills (`rule:6.5.4`). Sawmills generate wood tokens in Birdsong (`rule:6.4`). Not a Law contradiction, but a factual naming error.
- Riverfolk section refers to "A Better Burrow Bank"; the correct card name is Better Burrow Bank (`card:ROOT-55 (Base Game)`). The "A" prefix is not part of the card name.

## Stale or outdated

- Published November 2018; predates Underworld (2020), Marauder (2022), and Homeland (2024) expansions. Any "all factions" advice applies only to base + Riverfolk.
- Vagabond "Wolf vagabond" and "Beaver" are not official p16 character names. The base-game characters are Tinker, Thief, and Ranger; expansion characters include Adventurer, Harrier, and Ronin (`faction:vagabond$9.3.1`). These appear to be community or earlier-edition nicknames.
- Vagabond "pacifist building to reputation 3 for aide-stacking" — "reputation" is not a p16 term; the current Law uses "Relationships" and "Allied" status (`faction:vagabond$9.2.9`). "Aide-stacking" is also non-standard. The strategic concept of farming Aid for relationship improvement is correct, but the terminology is obsolete.
- Lizard Cult note "Recent rules changes improved faction viability significantly" — likely refers to pre-2020 revisions; correct as historical context, stale as current framing.

## Strategic judgment notes

- Woodland Alliance "Aim for 4–5 officers by endgame (3 is insufficient, excess limits warriors available)" — the [Woodland Alliance profile](../../factions/woodland.md) identifies 3+ officers as the inflection point and 4 as the point of no return. "3 is insufficient" is somewhat overstated but directionally consistent.
- Eyrie "Slot two cards in the decree on turn one" — the Decree rule requires adding *at least one* card per Birdsong, with a maximum of two (only one of which may be a bird) (`faction:eyrie$7.4.2`). Slotting two is a common recommendation but is not required; the source presents it as the default opening without flagging the minimum-one rule.
- Riverfolk "Eyrie and Lizard Cult are primary early customers" — the [Riverfolk profile](../../factions/riverfolk.md) identifies Marquise, Eyrie, and Underground Duchy as typical buyers. Lizard Cult is not flagged there as a primary customer; Marquise is omitted here. Judgment call; not a Law error.
- Cult "Target clearings with multiple building slots for maximum draw-symbol accumulation" — the draw bonuses are on the Gardens tracks (uncovered by placing gardens on the map), not tied directly to the number of building slots in any given clearing (`faction:cult$10.6.3`). More building slots per clearing can enable more garden placements in fewer clearings, but the framing "maximum draw-symbol accumulation" mislocates where those bonuses originate. Borderline factual — not a Law contradiction but misleading.

## Citations from Source — flagged

- Cult scoring: "Score by discarding cards matching suit of gardens on board. Dual requirement: gardens on board AND matching cards in hand" — the dual-requirement framing is correct (`faction:cult$10.5.3`), but the description implies the VP reward scales with garden presence rather than with the Gardens track position. Per `faction:cult$10.5.3`, scoring pays the VP printed above the *rightmost empty* Gardens track space of the matching suit — a single fixed track-value amount per suit per turn, not a function of how many gardens are on the map directly. More gardens placed does advance the track (uncovering higher-value spaces), but the per-Score VP is a track value, not "per garden." The source's wording does not explicitly say "per garden," but it omits the track-value mechanism entirely. See project memory entry on this common misstatement.

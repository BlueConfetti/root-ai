# Distilled

## General

This guide focuses on faction lore and narrative identity rather than strategy. It describes the Woodland setting and the ideological conflict between each faction. Strategic content is minimal — the value is contextual framing for new players.

---

## Marquise de Cat

Arrives from a distant empire during the Eyrie collapse. Establishes militaristic industrial control through workshops, lumber mills, and garrisons. Mixed consequences — some denizens benefit from industrialization, others experience oppression. This disparity fuels the Woodland Alliance's emergence.

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

Bird rulers claiming ancestral dominion through bureaucratic governance. The Great Civil War fragmented their authority, creating the power vacuum the Marquise exploits. Now re-unified, declaring total war to reclaim their domain.

---

## Woodland Alliance

Grassroots resistance emerging from collective oppression under Eyrie and Marquise. Decentralized network using sabotage, propaganda, and guerrilla warfare. Internal tension between violent and non-violent methods persists.

---

## Vagabond

Social outsiders operating beyond clearing structures. Survive as mercenaries, spies, saboteurs. Strategic position as wild cards — leverage independence to negotiate between factions.

---

## Riverfolk Company

River-dwelling merchants controlling trade routes through waterway monopolies. Calculated neutrality as intermediaries. Economic leverage rather than military power.

---

## Lizard Cult

Missionary faction spreading devotion to the Great Dragon. Expands quietly through theological persuasion rather than conquest. Hierarchical priesthood combines spiritual authority with political control.

---

## Underground Duchy

Mole civilization emerging from underground strongholds. Engineered fortifications, surprise-attack tunnel networks. Presents itself as alternative to surface chaos.

---

## Corvid Conspiracy

Shadowy intelligence network employing espionage, sabotage, misinformation, and assassination. Decentralization as goal — not ideologically motivated. Organized under a Mastermind directing regional cells.

---

## Keepers in Iron

Order recovering historical artifacts and preserving cultural heritage. Dangerous expeditions seeking relics and ancient knowledge. Fortified sanctuaries as repositories and training grounds.

---

## Lord of the Hundreds

Warlord-led warband unifying marauders through messianic devotion. Pursuit of total Woodland dominance through overwhelming force. Hierarchical — offers marginalized communities purpose and protection.

# Key Takeaways

- **Faction identities**: Each faction represents a distinct ideological position in the Woodland's power struggle.
- **Army/police factions** (Marquise, Eyrie): Established powers defending existing order or reclaiming authority.
- **Insurgent factions** (Woodland Alliance, Vagabond): Outside the formal power structure, winning through presence and disruption.
- **Economic factions** (Riverfolk): Neutral intermediaries profiting from conflict.
- **Religious/cultural factions** (Lizard Cult, Keepers): Ideology-driven expansion.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 8/10
**Verdict:** Entirely a lore/narrative primer with no mechanical claims to contradict the Law; minor flavour embellishments and one thematic mis-categorization (Vagabond as "insurgent") are the only findings.

## Verified claims

- All ten faction descriptions are broadly consistent with the canonical overviews in `rule:6.` through `rule:15.` (Marquise through Keepers in Iron). No faction's core identity is misrepresented.
- "Marquise establishes militaristic industrial control through workshops, lumber mills, and garrisons" — accurate to `rule:6.` overview (buildings are workshops, sawmills, recruiters; warriors garrison clearings).
- "Eyrie score points passively from roosts" is not stated explicitly, but the Eyrie paragraph describes them as "re-unified, declaring total war" — consistent with the Eyrie overview's "restore their once-dignified kind to their former glory" framing in `rule:7.`.
- "Riverfolk — Economic leverage rather than military power" and "Calculated neutrality as intermediaries" — consistent with the Riverfolk overview in `rule:11.`.
- "Cult — Hierarchical priesthood combines spiritual authority with political control" — consistent with `rule:10.` lore framing.
- Keepers description as "Order recovering historical artifacts" is consistent with `rule:15.` overview ("recover relics lost in past conflicts").

## Strategic judgment notes

- Vagabond is labeled an "Insurgent faction" in Key Takeaways alongside the Woodland Alliance. The `rule:9.` Vagabond overview and the [Vagabond profile](../../factions/vagabond.md) characterise the Vagabond as a neutral independent agent operating "beyond clearing structures" — not an ideological insurgent. The Alliance/Vagabond pairing is defensible as a thematic "outsider" grouping, but the "insurgent" label creates a misleading parallel with the Alliance's explicitly anti-establishment identity. Acceptable as lore framing with this caveat.
- "Great Civil War fragmented [Eyrie] authority" — the `rule:7.` overview says "restore their once-dignified kind to their former glory" but mentions no specific named war. This is fan/flavour elaboration beyond the canonical Law text, not a mechanical claim.
- "Corvid — Organized under a Mastermind directing regional cells" — the `rule:13.` Corvid overview mentions "subterfuge and extortion" and criminal plots; no Mastermind figure appears in the Law. Creative embellishment.
- Duchy "surprise-attack tunnel networks" — tunnels in `rule:12.` are adjacency connectors (the Burrow) and surface entry points, not specifically characterised as surprise-attack tools. Flavour expansion.
- Keepers presented as heroically "preserving cultural heritage" — the `rule:15.` overview explicitly raises moral ambiguity ("whether these relics belong to the Keepers or the Woodland … is another question"). The source omits this nuance. Not a mechanical error, but a one-sided lore reading.

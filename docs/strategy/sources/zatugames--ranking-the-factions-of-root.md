## Marquise de Cat

Strong early game with abundant warriors and excellent combat (9/10) and board control (9/10), but poor action economy undermines long-term viability. Winability rated 4/10 — military dominance alone does not translate to victory.

---

## Eyrie Dynasties

Powerful Decree provides numerous actions but turmoil risks are significant. Winability 6/10. Ranked 6th overall.

---

## Woodland Alliance

"Strongest defense of any faction" — takes the higher die roll when defending. Winability 9/10. Ranked 2nd overall.

---

## Vagabond

Single-piece faction. Once set up with needed items, very little opponents can do to stop progression. Winability 7/10. Ranked 5th — ideal for newcomers.

---

## Riverfolk Company

Controls river trade through economic leverage rather than military force. Moderately winnable. Ranked 7th.

---

## Lizard Cult

Complex mechanics (ease 3/10) but extremely powerful once mastered. Winability 9/10. Ranked 3rd.

---

## Underground Duchy

Top-ranked overall: perfect 10/10 combat and winability. "Strong military force combined with consistent point scoring makes this faction the easiest to win reliably with."

---

## Corvid Conspiracy

Limited board presence despite explosive tactical options. Difficult to execute successfully. Ranked 9th.

---

## Lord of the Hundreds

Most aggressive faction. Unmatched military strength. 10/10 combat rating. Winability 8/10. Ranked 4th.

---

## Keepers in Iron

Extremely difficult to master. Poor combat. Limited opponent interaction. Ranked last (#10).

# Key Takeaways

- **Underground Duchy ranked #1**: Consistent military + scoring engine; highest overall viability per this author.
- **Woodland Alliance #2**: Defense advantage (higher die roll) plus 9/10 winability.
- **Lizard Cult #3**: Hardest to learn (3/10 ease) but near-highest winability once mastered.
- **Marquise has strong combat but low winability (4/10)**: Military dominance does not translate to VP victory.
- **Keepers in Iron ranked last**: Difficult to master, unreliable mechanics — not recommended for casual play.
- **Vagabond is newcomer-friendly**: High ease, isolated from early aggression, inevitable once set up.
- **Lord of the Hundreds is the brute-force option**: 10/10 combat, 8/10 winability — viable for aggressive players.

# Review

**Reviewed:** 2026-04-29 by `root-3p-reviewer`
**Accuracy rating:** 7/10
**Verdict:** One verifiable mechanical claim (Woodland Alliance Guerrilla War) is correct; all other claims are pure strategic opinion with no Law contradictions found.

## Verified claims

- "Takes the higher die roll when defending" (Woodland Alliance) ✓ matches `faction:woodland$8.2.2` (Guerrilla War): "the Alliance will deal hits equal to the higher roll, and the attacker will deal hits equal to the lower roll."
- "Unreliable action and relics" (Keepers in Iron) ✓ consistent with the retinue discard-check mechanic at `rule:15.5.2` — the Law requires discarding a Retinue card when the ruled-clearings count falls below the relic's value during Delve and Recover.
- "Turmoil risks are significant" (Eyrie) ✓ consistent with `rule:7.7` — Turmoil causes VP loss per bird card on the Decree, purges the Decree, and deposes the leader.
- "Controls river trade through economic leverage" (Riverfolk) ✓ consistent with `rule:11.` — the Riverfolk sell three services (Hand Card, Riverboats, Mercenaries) and score dividends from funds plus VP from trade post placement.

## Strategic judgment notes

- "Strongest defense of any faction" (Woodland Alliance) — defensible given Guerrilla War, but it is an opinion. The [Woodland Alliance profile](../../factions/woodland.md) does not use this superlative; it describes Guerrilla War as making attacking "statistically miserable" rather than uniquely strongest. Pass through as opinion.
- "Very little anyone can do to stop you once set up" (Vagabond) — overstated. The [Vagabond profile](../../factions/vagabond.md) identifies several meaningful threats: Alliance revolts damaging three items via Full Removal, item depletion under sustained Hostility, Corvid Snares blocking quest clearings, and Hundreds razing ruins to cut item supply. The Vagabond's pawn is removal-immune but not invulnerable — item damage can cripple the engine. Acceptable as casual-player characterization; not accurate as a strategic assessment.
- Rankings (Underground Duchy #1, Lizard Cult #3, Marquise 4/10 winability, etc.) — all subjective; no Law-derivable fact to verify. No position taken.
